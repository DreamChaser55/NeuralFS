VEGA REQUIEM
A FreeSpace 1 Campaign
Campaign Bible — Final Reference Document

TITLE: VEGA REQUIEM
TAGLINE: "They named the system for a dying star. It was an omen no one chose to read."
MISSION COUNT: 6 missions, 2 acts
TONE: Survival horror, military tragedy, desperate last stand, dark elegiac close
POV: GTA Terran pilot, Alpha 1, 79th "Iron Wolves" Squadron, GTD Amadeus (GTD Orion-class)
CANON STANCE: Canon-compliant side-story. Depicts the destruction of the GTD Amadeus in the Vega
system, an event implied by FS1 Command Briefing 1.5 (January 25, 2335).

===============================================================================
TIMELINE PLACEMENT
===============================================================================

January 15–24, 2335 — the ten days immediately before the GTA's formal declaration of war against
the Shivans (FS1 Command Briefing 1.5, January 25, 2335).

Key canon anchors:

  FS1 CB 1.5 (1/25/2335):
  "After the 3rd Fleet lost the GTD Amadeus in the Vega system, the GTA and the Parliament of
  Vasuda have both declared open states of war against the Shivans."
  → This campaign depicts that loss in full.

  FS1 CB 1.3 (1/18/2335):
  "The Shivans have inflicted heavy casualties to both Terran and Vasudan forces in the Beta Cygni
  and Vega systems."
  → This directly references the events of this campaign's first act.

  FS1 CB 2.2 (2/15/2335):
  "The Hammer of Light has decimated the Vasudan fleet at Vega."
  → This references a subsequent, larger Vasudan counterattack—after Terran forces have already
  evacuated. Our Mission 1-3 depicts an earlier Vasudan fleet element meeting the same fate.

===============================================================================
SYSTEMS AND NODE ROUTES
===============================================================================

Primary Theater: Vega system
  - Confirmed node connections (per official map): Vega → Antares, Beta Aquilae, Capella, Deneb
  - Retreat route used in campaign: Vega → Antares (final escape vector)
  - Shivan entry vectors: Vega → Capella node (primary) and Vega → Deneb node (secondary)
  - No unstable or uncharted nodes used. All connections are from the official node map.

Vega is strategically critical: it is a four-node hub connecting Terran core space (via Antares and
Beta Aquilae) to outer systems (Capella, Deneb). Its loss isolates those approaches entirely.

ERA MECHANICS AND CONSTRAINTS
===============================================================================

Available weapons by mission:

  Missions 1-1 and 1-2 (Jan 15–17):
    Primaries: GTW ML-16 Laser only
    Secondaries: GTM MX-50, GTM Fury
    Note: Avenger cannon exists as a prototype but is not mass-deployed. GTA pilots are equipped
    with the same 14-Year-War loadout that proved inadequate against Shivan shields. This is the
    core horror of Act 1: familiar weapons are almost useless.

  Mission 1-3 (Jan 20):
    Primaries: GTW ML-16, GTW-15 Avenger (field-test units — player lead only in this mission)
    Secondaries: GTM MX-50, GTM Fury
    Note: The Amadeus received a small allocation of field-modified Avenger cannons. The player
    gets one; wingmen still carry ML-16. First time Shivan shields feel real resistance.

  Missions 2-1, 2-2, 2-3 (Jan 21–24):
    Primaries: GTW ML-16, GTW-15 Avenger (now standard for whole wing)
    Secondaries: GTM MX-50, GTM Fury
    Note: Full Avenger deployment reached the Amadeus just before Jan 21. GTA fighter shields are
    NOT yet available (CB 1.5, Jan 25 — one day after our campaign ends).

What is NOT available in this campaign (comes later in the main FS1 timeline):
  - GTW Flail (CB 2.1, Feb 11)
  - GTM Interceptor (CB 2.1, Feb 11)
  - GTM Phoenix V (CB 2.2, Feb 15)
  - GTM Hornet (CB 3.1, Mar 1)
  - GTM Stiletto / Tsunami / Harbinger (CB 1.6 and later)
  - GTB Athena (CB 1.6, Jan 31 — just after campaign ends)
  - GTB Medusa (CB 2.2), GTB Ursa (CB 3.2) — much later
  - Shivan fighter shields for GTA pilots (CB 1.5, Jan 25 — one day after campaign ends)
  - Intersystem jump drives for fighters (CB 3.2, Mar 1)
  - GTW Banshee (CB 3.1, Mar 1)
  - GTW Prometheus — available per era but not distributed to the Amadeus; not included
  - GTW Disruptor — available but not relevant (no capture objectives in this campaign)

Available ships:
  All missions: GTF Apollo
  Missions 1-2 onward: GTF Valkyrie (Amadeus received a complement on Jan 17, matching
                        the fleet-wide distribution referenced in FS1 CB 1.2)
  No bombers available throughout this campaign.

Capital ship rules:
  - No beam cannons (FS2 era tech)
  - No corvettes (FS2 era class)
  - No impervious capital ship shields (only the SD Lucifer possesses those; no Lucifer in Vega)
  - Shivan fighter/bomber shields are strong but not impervious; sustained ML-16 fire can kill,
    but Avenger significantly improves kill speed

===============================================================================
THEMES, PROTAGONIST ARC, ANTAGONIST MOTIVATIONS
===============================================================================

Themes:
  1. THE WAGES OF UNPREPAREDNESS — The GTA spent fourteen years perfecting how to kill Vasudans
     and built no doctrine for anything else. Vega burns because no one was ready for something new.
  2. SALVAGE OVER VICTORY — Every mission objective is about saving lives, not seizing ground.
     This campaign cannot be "won." It can only be survived, and how well you survive is measured
     in people, not positions.
  3. THE COST OF LOYALTY — The Admiral stays with his ship. Pilots die covering transports. The
     Vasudan commander sails knowingly into an ambush because retreat is not an option her orders
     allow. Loyalty in extremis is both the most noble and most ruinous thing people do.
  4. RELUCTANT KINSHIP — The Terran-Vasudan ceasefire isn't signed yet, but in the field,
     soldiers recognize their common enemy faster than their governments do. The cooperation in
     Mission 1-3 prefigures the alliance; it begins not with diplomacy but with shared terror.

Protagonist arc (Alpha 1, the player):
  Act 1, Missions 1-1 and 1-2: Combat veteran rendered helpless. Weapons bounce off the enemy.
  Standard formations dissolve. Friends die. There is no briefing that covers this scenario.
  Act 1, Mission 1-3: First adaptation. New weapon. New ally (the enemy, provisionally). The
  player has stopped reacting and started improvising.
  Act 2, Missions 2-1 and 2-2: Professionalism under grief. The ship is dying. The admiral
  won't leave. The pilot does the only thing available: keep the bombers off the hull as long as
  possible. Then protect the pods.
  Act 2, Mission 2-3: A survivor's mission. No glory. Just the jump node and whoever can be
  dragged through it.

Admiral Chen Wei arc:
  Introduced in CB 1.1 as a calm, methodical commander. Keeps the briefings steady even as the
  situation deteriorates. In Mission 2-2 his final order is to "hold position" — a euphemism that
  fools no one. Dies with the Amadeus. His personal log (fiction viewer, Mission 2-2) shows the
  human being under the rank.

Antagonist motivations:
  SHIVANS — Unknown and unknowable. They are not conquering territory or gathering resources.
  They attack without communication, without pattern any Terran mind can parse. They are a natural
  catastrophe with engines. The horror is not malice; it is indifference.
  HAMMER OF LIGHT — Fanatics operating from faith, not hatred. They believe the Shivans are cosmic
  preservers fulfilling prophecy. Betraying the Vasudan fleet is, to them, a holy act. They are
  terrifying not despite this belief but because of it.

===============================================================================
PLOTLINE SUMMARY
===============================================================================

January 15, 2335. The Vega system is a four-node hub and a symbol of GTA reach. The 3rd Fleet,
built around the GTD Amadeus, has garrisoned it through the final grinding months of the
Terran-Vasudan War.

The first attack comes without warning. GTT Meridian, a patrol transport, goes silent near the
Vega-Capella node. When Alpha wing investigates (Mission 1-1), they find wreckage and something
new: black fighter craft that barely register on sensors, shrug off laser fire, and kill with
inhuman precision. Three of Alpha's four ships make it back. Their sensor logs are the first
detailed Shivan combat record from the Vega system.

Two days later (Mission 1-2), the Shivans mass against GTI Vega Prime. Alpha wing holds the
fighter screen while Epsilon transports evacuate civilian and technical personnel. The station falls,
but most people escape. The GTC Stentor, intercepting the Shivan cruiser SC Malgor with suicidal
commitment, buys the transports the minutes they need.

By January 18, field-modified Avenger cannons begin reaching the Amadeus. The weapons finally
bite. But the Shivans don't stop coming. The Vasudan 9th Fleet advance element—PVD Vigil and her
escorts—enters Vega to fight the common enemy (Mission 1-3). Hammer of Light agents have already
sold their approach vector. The Vigil dies in seconds at the Capella node. Vasudan refugee
transports scatter toward Antares. Alpha wing helps them—no order required, no alliance yet
signed, just the choice of one pilot at one moment. The Vasudan wing commander sends a brief
transmission of thanks before going silent.

January 21. The retreat begins (Mission 2-1). The Amadeus, damaged in three days of constant
strikes, begins escorting surviving personnel ships toward Antares. Shivan bomber wings harry
the convoy. The SC Ahriman—a Lilith-class cruiser—begins its pursuit. The convoy holds together,
barely.

January 22. The Amadeus can no longer move effectively (Mission 2-2). Admiral Wei makes the
decision: the ship holds position at Nav Charlie and draws fire while her crew evacuates in escape
pods. He remains on the bridge. The player fights the most desperate battle of the campaign—
protecting nine escape pods from Dragon-class fighters and the Ahriman's guns while the Amadeus
burns. The ship dies. The Admiral goes with her.

January 24. Vega's last defenders converge on the Antares node (Mission 2-3). The SC Malgor
sits at the node with a fighter screen. GTC Stentor, four freighters, two Vasudan transports.
There are SF Dragons at the node. Stentor takes a direct hit and Commander Okafor orders emergency
transit. Several freighters don't make it. Enough do.

The Vega-Antares node goes dark behind them.

The Vega system, GTA designation VL-001, fell to the Shivans on January 24, 2335.

ACT STRUCTURE
===============================================================================

ACT 1: THE SHIVAN TIDE (3 missions, January 15–20, 2335)
  Beat arc: Denial → shock → grim recognition
  Opening state: Routine garrison assignment, Terran-Vasudan War winding down
  Closing state: Station lost, Vasudan fleet ambushed, retreat now inevitable
  Emotional register: Horror, disbelief, improvisation

  CB 1.1 (before Mission 1-1): Routine briefing. No mention of Shivans (not yet confirmed).
  Mission 1-1 "Strange Fires": First contact. Weapons don't work. Barely survive.
  Mission 1-2 "Breakwater": Station defense. Evacuate civilians. Watch Vega Prime die.
    Fiction Viewer: Station technician's last minutes aboard GTI Vega Prime
  CB 1.2 (before Mission 1-3): Shivans confirmed. Avengers arriving. Vasudan fleet incoming.
  Mission 1-3 "No Truce With the Tide": Vasudan fleet ambushed. Player helps them escape.
    Fiction Viewer: PVD Vigil's tactical officer—final approach and ambush

ACT 2: NO WAY HOME (3 missions, January 21–24, 2335)
  Beat arc: Hope → loss → hard-won survival
  Opening state: Ordered withdrawal underway, Amadeus damaged but moving
  Closing state: Amadeus destroyed, Admiral dead, survivors through the Antares node
  Emotional register: Grief, professionalism, sacrifice

  CB 2.1 (before Mission 2-1): Ordered withdrawal. Shivans control 3 of 4 Vega nodes.
  Mission 2-1 "Dead Reckoning": Convoy escort. Bomber intercept. SC Ahriman appears.
  Mission 2-2 "The Last Full Measure": Amadeus holds position. Protect escape pods.
    Fiction Viewer: Admiral Chen Wei's personal log—the night before
  Mission 2-3 "Vega Requiem": Final breakthrough at the Antares node.
    Fiction Viewer: Omniscient reflection—a system going dark

CONTINUITY NOTES
===============================================================================

  - GTD Amadeus destruction is canonical per FS1 CB 1.5. This campaign depicts that event directly.
  - CB 1.3 reference to "heavy casualties... in the Vega system" encompasses this campaign's events.
  - The HoL decimation of the Vasudan fleet at Vega (CB 2.2) refers to a later, larger Vasudan
    counterattack after Terran forces have evacuated. Our Mission 1-3 depicts an earlier, smaller
    fleet element meeting the same fate. No contradiction.
  - The Terran-Vasudan ceasefire is NOT in effect during this campaign (signed CB 1.5, Jan 25).
    All Terran-Vasudan cooperation here is ad-hoc and battlefield-driven.
  - GTA fighter shields become available January 25 (CB 1.5). Our campaign ends January 24.
    No GTA fighter shields in any mission.
  - Avenger cannon mass deployment: CB 1.5, Jan 25. Our depiction of limited field units reaching
    the Amadeus by Jan 20 is a plausible pre-deployment to frontline units. Narratively established
    in CB 1.2.
  - All Vega node connections used are confirmed on the official node map.
  - Admiral Chen Wei, Commander Yael Okafor, GTC Stentor, GTI Vega Prime, SC Malgor, and SC Ahriman
    are all original creations. No canon-named officers are assigned to the GTD Amadeus.
  - "SC Malgor" and "SC Ahriman" follow the convention that Shivan ship names are GTA designations,
    not names the Shivans use themselves.
  - No SD Demon, SD Lucifer, or superdestroyer class appears in this campaign. The drama is
    sustained by cruisers and fighter wings, maintaining scale appropriate to a 6-mission campaign.
  - The GTF Valkyrie availability from Mission 1-2 onward is consistent with CB 1.2's fleet-wide
    distribution announcement on January 15.
