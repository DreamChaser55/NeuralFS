================================================================================
COMMAND BRIEFING 2.1
"Ordered Withdrawal"
================================================================================

Delivered aboard: GTD Amadeus
Location: Vega System — deep space, inner zone
Date: January 21, 2335

--- STAGE 1: SYSTEM STATUS ---
This is Rear Admiral Chen Wei. Listen carefully. I'm not going to spend time softening this.

Vega is lost.

I want to be precise about what that means. We have not been defeated in open battle. We have
been systematically dismantled by an enemy whose capabilities exceed ours in every measurable
category, and whose intentions we still do not fully understand. GTI Vega Prime is gone. The
Shivans control the Capella and Deneb node approaches with capital ship elements. The Beta
Aquilae approach has not yet been probed, but I expect that within forty-eight hours.

That leaves the Antares node. That is our exit.

I am ordering an organized withdrawal of all GTA assets from Vega system, effective immediately.
I want to be equally precise about what "organized" means: we leave together, we leave in order,
and we do not leave anyone behind who can be brought out.

--- STAGE 2: WITHDRAWAL DISPOSITION ---
The GTD Amadeus will lead the withdrawal convoy, with GTC Stentor on the flank. We are
escorting five ships: GTFr Aldrin One, Two, and Three — military freighters carrying sensor
equipment and ground station salvage from Vega Prime — GTT Meridian's Rescue (a transport
carrying Meridian's surviving crew, recovered from the escape pods you brought back), and the
Vasudan transport Theta Three. Theta One and Two made good their jump from Vega in Mission
Twelve. Theta Three had a drive fault that delayed her transit. She's been holding here under
our observation for two days. She comes out with us.

I expect objections. I do not have time for objections. The ceasefire is being formalized as we
speak. Theta Three is under my protection as commanding officer of this convoy and I am not going
to abandon a transport full of Vasudan civilians to whatever is waiting at the Capella and Deneb
nodes. That is final.

--- STAGE 3: THREAT ASSESSMENT ---
Shivan pressure has increased since yesterday. We're tracking fighter wings in the inner system.
More concerning: we have a Lilith-class cruiser on our sensors, designated SC Ahriman, which has
been shadowing the Amadeus at long range since early this morning. The Ahriman has not committed
to a direct engagement. I believe she's waiting for us to begin our transit — more exposed, less
maneuverable, the freighters slowing us down.

I believe correctly. Expect the Ahriman to become an active problem as soon as we move.

Alpha wing's assignment is to run fighter intercept ahead of the convoy. The bombers are our
greatest vulnerability. Keep Shivan bomber wings off the Amadeus and the transports. The Stentor
will engage the Ahriman if she closes; that is not your problem. Your problem is everything with
wings.

--- STAGE 4: CONTINGENCY ---
If the convoy sustains losses that prevent safe transit before we reach the node, I will hold at
Nav Bravo and reassess. This is not something I anticipate. I also do not anticipate many things
that have happened in the last six days.

Report to your wing stations immediately. We move in fifteen minutes.

MISSION DESIGN DOCUMENT
Mission 2-1: "Dead Reckoning"
================================================================================

PURPOSE AND PLAYER EXPERIENCE
- Mission fantasy: The player is the rearguard of a convoy in retreat. Every fighter kill is a
  ship that doesn't get to the convoy. Every bomber that slips through is damage the Amadeus
  can't sustain. The convoy grinds forward and the player grinds alongside it.
- Core tension: The SC Ahriman's presence is constant and threatening but the player cannot
  engage it — the Stentor is handling it. The player must resist the urge to deal with the
  "real" threat and focus on the bomber waves that are actually within their reach. This tests
  fire discipline and mission focus.
- Plot advance: The retreat is underway. The Amadeus is damaged. The Ahriman begins a pursuit
  that will culminate in Mission 2-2. The convoy loses some assets but survives as a unit.
  The player learns what they're fighting to protect.
- Emotional register: Professional and grim. This is not a defense — it's a withdrawal. There
  is no glory here, only competence against relentless pressure.
- This mission feels like escorting a wounded animal out of hunting grounds while the predator
  circles. Everything is slower. Everything is harder. And the destination is still far away.

FICTION VIEWER NARRATIVE
- Displayed before this mission.
- File: Mission_2_1_story.txt
- Summary: The story of Captain Elias Vance aboard GTFr Aldrin One, navigating the dense asteroid field blindly due to sensor interference, relying entirely on Alpha wing's comms chatter.
- Purpose: Emphasizes the terror and claustrophobia of the asteroid field, as well as the absolute trust the vulnerable civilian and logistical crews must place in the player's hands.

ENVIRONMENT AND SETUP
- Datestamp: January 21, 2335
- System: Vega — inner system, en route from center-system position to Antares node
- Location: Moving convoy. The scene progresses through a dense, hazardous asteroid field, with Nav Bravo as the goal.
- Background: The convoy is navigating a treacherous asteroid belt (remnant of the dying Vega star's early ejections). Asteroids provide physical cover but pose navigational hazards. Sensor interference reduces radar range, creating a claustrophobic, horror-survival atmosphere. Darker in color register, punctuated by asteroid shadows.
- Scene layout:
  - GTD Amadeus: Present and moving. Damaged (hull at approximately 60%). Escort formation.
    Moving at reduced speed (engine damage). Turrets operational.
  - GTC Stentor: On the starboard flank of the Amadeus, between the convoy and the Ahriman.
    Hull: ~70% (running repairs since Mission 1-2). Stentor AI is handling Ahriman at long range.
  - GTFr Aldrin 1, 2, 3 (GTFr Chronos-class): Freighters in loose formation, ahead of the Amadeus.
    Each carries salvaged sensor equipment and Vega Prime administrative data. All unarmed.
    Speed: Slow. They set the convoy's pace.
  - GTT Rescue (GTT Elysium-class): Transport carrying Meridian survivors. Positioned centrally.
  - PVT Theta 3 (PVT Isis-class): The Vasudan transport with the drive fault. Positioned at rear
    of convoy. Slightly faster than the freighters but slower than the Amadeus would normally be.
  - Nav Bravo: 10,000m from convoy start position, directly ahead along established corridor.
  - Nav Alpha: Midpoint waypoint, 5,000m from start. Convoy must pass through.
  - SC Ahriman (SC Lilith-class): In scene at mission start, 4,000m to the flank-aft. Moving in
    loose parallel. Closing slowly to engage the Stentor. The Ahriman will maintain a tactical
    distance of about 2,000–3,000m, providing a spectacular close-quarters plasma broadside exchange.
  - GTS Casca: Present, moving with the convoy. Available for rearming. Positioned near Amadeus.
    Casca is fragile. If Shivan fighters acquire it, it will die quickly.

PARTICIPANTS

Allied:
- Alpha wing: GTF Valkyrie x4 (Alpha 1 = player, Alpha 2–4 = AI)
  - Loadout: GTW-15 Avenger (bank 1), GTW ML-16 Laser (bank 2); GTM MX-50 (24), GTM Fury (16)
  - Full Avenger deployment — whole wing has Avengers now. This is the "new normal."
  - Initial orders: Escort GTD Amadeus; intercept hostile bombers; protect GTFr Aldrin wing

- Beta wing: GTF Apollo x4 (AI-controlled — 14th Hardpoints squadron)
  - Loadout: GTW-15 Avenger x2; GTM MX-50 (24)
  - Initial orders: Escort GTT Rescue; cover convoy rear
  - Beta serves as a backup defensive screen. They're competent but not available to the player
    as directed reinforcements — they're tasked separately.

- GTD Amadeus (GTD Orion-class): Present, moving, engaged by SC Ahriman at long range
  - Hull: 60% at start. Must survive to reach Nav Bravo.
  - The Amadeus's own turrets engage any Shivan fighter or bomber that comes within 3 km.
  - The Amadeus's maneuverability is severely limited by engine damage.

- GTC Stentor (GTC Fenris-class): On starboard flank, engaging SC Ahriman
  - Hull: 70%. Fighting the Ahriman at range. She's holding her own but taking damage.
  - Commander Okafor manages this fight independently. She does not request Alpha's help.
    Okafor has been told: keep Ahriman busy. She intends to do exactly that.
  - Stentor hull will decrease through the mission. If she falls below 30%, she begins a
    fighting withdrawal rather than closing. This is her survival instinct — she needs to
    survive too, for Mission 2-3. Design note: Stentor hull at end of this mission carries over
    to Mission 2-3 as narrative consequence (no mechanic — stated in CB 2.2).

- GTFr Aldrin 1, 2, 3 (GTFr Chronos-class): Convoy freighters. Primary non-combat assets.
  - Chronos class has some hull strength and no weapons.
  - Speed: Slow but not impossibly so. They move at Amadeus-reduced-speed pace.
  - Each carries irreplaceable data — losing them is a tactical cost noted in debrief.

- GTT Rescue (GTT Elysium-class): Transport. Unarmed. Must survive.

- PVT Theta 3 (PVT Isis-class): Vasudan transport. Has 3 turrets (fires at Shivans in range).
  - Her presence is Admiral Wei's choice. She is an emotional anchor: the player knows her from
    Mission 1-3. "She's still here" is its own kind of relief.

- GTS Casca (GTS Centaur-class): Moving with convoy. Available for rearming.

Hostile:
- Indra wing: SF Scorpion x4
  - Arrives from ahead (toward Antares — Shivans are in front too) at T+01:30, 10 km
  - Initial orders: Attack Alpha wing (fighter intercept role — keep player busy)
  - These fighters want to draw Alpha away from the convoy so the bombers have an open run.

- Kali wing: SB Shaitan x3 (bomber wave 1)
  - Arrives from the Capella/aft direction at T+02:30, 12 km
  - Initial orders: Attack GTD Amadeus (primary), Attack convoy freighters (secondary)
  - Three Shaitans targeting the Amadeus is the mission's central crisis. Player must choose:
    deal with Indra (who's fighting the wing) or break off to intercept Kali (who targets command).

- Deva wing: SF Basilisk x3 (bomber escort)
  - Arrives with Kali wing at T+02:30, 12 km
  - Initial orders: Escort Kali wing; attack Alpha wing if attacked
  - The escort complicates the bomber intercept. Player can't just dive on Kali — Deva will
    hit them while they do.

- Genma wing: SB Shaitan x3 (bomber wave 2)
  - Arrives from the aft at T+06:00 or when Kali wing is destroyed (whichever comes SECOND —
    this is the escalation rule; the second wave doesn't come until the first is handled)
  - Initial orders: Attack GTT Rescue (primary), Attack GTFr Aldrin (secondary)
  - The second wave shifts from the Amadeus to the softer convoy targets. Targeting the transport
    carrying survivors adds emotional weight to the intercept decision.

- SC Ahriman (SC Lilith-class): Present throughout, engaging GTC Stentor at range
  - The Ahriman is NOT a player target in this mission. She is a contextual threat — visible,
    dangerous to the Stentor, drawing fire — but outside Alpha wing's assignment.
  - Ahriman is substantially more dangerous than the Malgor (Cain-class) — Lilith is the heavy
    cruiser variant with stronger weapons. The player can observe this difference in the Stentor
    battle.
  - If the player attempts to attack the Ahriman, Command issues a recall order ("Not your target,
    Alpha Lead. Leave the Ahriman to the Stentor"). The Ahriman's cluster bomb will trigger if a
    fighter closes within 400m, which is very dangerous.

PLAYER CRAFT AND LOADOUT
- Ship: GTF Valkyrie (recommended for intercept role) or GTF Apollo
- Primary: GTW-15 Avenger (bank 1), GTW ML-16 (bank 2) — fully standard now
- Secondary: GTM MX-50 (24), GTM Fury (16)
- Support: GTS Casca (always available — moving with convoy)
- Reinforcements: None callable. Beta wing is on separate tasking.

OBJECTIVES AND DIRECTIVES

Primary:
  - GTD Amadeus reaches Nav Bravo (convoy endpoint).

Secondary:
  - At least 3 of 5 convoy ships (Aldrin 1/2/3, Rescue, Theta 3) survive to Nav Bravo.
  - Surgical Strike: Disable SC Ahriman's forward beam/weapons subsystem to buy the convoy time.

Bonus:
  - All 5 convoy ships survive. (Requires both bomber waves intercepted cleanly.)
  - SC Ahriman hull reduced below 50% by end of mission. (Bonus only — achieved by Stentor in
    most cases, but player can contribute briefly if situation allows.)

Failure conditions:
  - GTD Amadeus destroyed.
  - (Alpha 1 destroyed = standard.)

EVENT LOGIC SKETCH
- "Indra wing arrival" fires at T+01:30 unconditionally.
- "Kali and Deva arrival" fires at T+02:30 unconditionally.
- "Genma arrival" fires when both: Kali wing ships-alive = 0 AND T+06:00 is passed. If Kali
  is still alive after T+06:00, Genma waits until Kali is destroyed. This prevents the player
  from being overwhelmed simultaneously by both bomber waves.
- "Ahriman engages Stentor": AI goal set at mission start. Stentor returns fire. Both ships
  will engage in a running plasma broadside exchange at 2,000–3,000m range.
- "Command warns about Ahriman distraction": fires if player's Alpha 1 ship is within 3 km of
  SC Ahriman. One-time trigger.
- "Mission success" condition: GTD Amadeus arrives within 2,000m of Nav Bravo.
- "Mission failure" condition: GTD Amadeus hull drops to 0%.
- Secondary "convoy survivors" count: tracked, reported in debrief. At least 3 of 5 needed.
- Casca AI: follows Amadeus, available for rearming on request at any time.

NEWLY INTRODUCED SHIPS AND WEAPONS
- SC Ahriman (SC Lilith-class): First Lilith in campaign. Noticeably more dangerous than the
  Cain-class SC Malgor encountered in Act 1. Her presence as an unkillable, persistent threat
  is a design escalation from the Malgor (which was also unkillable but less visually menacing).
- GTFr Chronos (GTFr Aldrin wing): Named convoy freighters, emotionally grounded through
  dialogue and the stated contents (irreplaceable data, survivors from Meridian).
- GTT Rescue: Direct emotional callback to Mission 1-1 (the Meridian survivors). Players who
  remember the pods they saved now have a reason to protect this particular transport.
- SB Shaitan x2 waves: Most sustained Shaitan engagement of the campaign so far. Tests player
  bomber-intercept competence.

DEBRIEFINGS

Full success (Amadeus reached Nav Bravo, all 5 convoy ships survived):
The convoy is at Nav Bravo. All five ships made it.
I want to note what that means in terms of what was at stake: Aldrin One and Two are carrying
the complete sensor database from Vega Prime. We didn't lose a sensor record. GTT Rescue has
every surviving crew member from the Meridian. Theta Three is intact.
The Stentor is damaged, but she held the Ahriman at range. Commander Okafor did her job
exactly as planned.
We know the Ahriman is still shadowing us. We'll deal with that when it becomes unavoidable.
Good flying, Alpha. Tomorrow will be harder.

Partial success (Amadeus reached Nav Bravo, 3–4 convoy ships survived):
Convoy reached Nav Bravo. We lost [1 or 2 ships] on the transit.
[If Aldrin ship lost]: The sensor data from that Chronos is gone. We couldn't help that, but the
loss is real. [If Rescue lost]: The Meridian survivors — the people from those pods in Mission
One — are gone. I don't have words for that. We proceed.
The Ahriman is still with us. She'll be a problem at the node. Rest while you can.

Failure (Amadeus destroyed):
The Amadeus did not survive the transit. Without the Amadeus, there is no convoy, no organized
withdrawal. Whatever makes it to Antares will scatter.
This is the worst outcome of this operation. The Ahriman's pressure, the bomber waves — one
of them found the limit.

QA NOTES
- Convoy speed must be balanced carefully: fast enough that the mission doesn't stall waiting
  for the Amadeus to reach Nav Bravo, slow enough that bomber threats feel relevant for more
  than a brief window.
- "Genma fires AFTER Kali is destroyed" rule is critical for balance. If both waves are active
  simultaneously the player faces 6 Shaitans at once, which is unmanageable.
- The Ahriman must remain at standoff distance from the convoy. She should not close to within
  2 km of the Amadeus — that would either cause instant mission failure or require the player to
  engage a Lilith-class cruiser alone, which is not the intent. Ahriman's AI goal: maintain range
  of 2,000–3,000m from GTC Stentor while engaging her.
- GTS Casca is the mission's safety valve. Players who get overrun by fighters and bombers should
  be able to call Casca and recover. Casca's positioning near the Amadeus means it's always
  somewhat protected.
- The Admiral's calm radio message is a tonal design note: he's a composed commander, not a
  panicking one. He has one calm line when his ship takes damage. This models the right emotional
  register for the mission — grim and professional, not hysterical.
- Beta wing on independent tasking: they should be visible and active (protecting GTT Rescue from
  stray fighters) but not overlapping the player's objectives. They reduce the player's load
  slightly without trivializing the challenge.
