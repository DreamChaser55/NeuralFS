================================================================================
MISSION DESIGN DOCUMENT
Mission 2-3: "Vega Requiem"
================================================================================

PURPOSE AND PLAYER EXPERIENCE
- Mission fantasy: The last survivors of the Vega garrison must break through a Shivan blocking
  force at the Antares node and jump out. This is a sprint, not a battle. The player is not here
  to win; they are here to survive and bring as many people as possible through the door before
  it closes behind them.
- Core tension: SC Malgor sits at the node. SF Dragons at the blocking screen. The convoy
  contains GTC Stentor, four freighters, two Vasudan transports, and the escape pods from
  Mission 2-2 (now aboard the Stentor). The transit through the node is not safe — ships will
  take fire as they jump. Some may not make it. The player fights to minimize those losses.
- Plot advance: The system falls. The jump node goes dark behind the last survivors. The
  campaign ends not with triumph but with the sound of a closing door.
- Emotional register: Grief, exhaustion, and the survivor's particular kind of determination.
  The fiction viewer has already told the player what this system meant. The debrief is what
  happens after the door closes — not a victory speech, but a final accounting.
- Design note: This is the hardest logistical challenge and the most emotionally restrained mission.
  The player has seen enough. The game knows it. Let the mission be efficient and the debrief
  carry the weight.

FICTION VIEWER NARRATIVE
- Displayed before this mission.
- File: Mission_2_3_story.txt
- Summary: An omniscient meditation on the Vega system — its name, its history, the colony that
  was there, the nodes that connected it, and what happens to a system when no one comes back to
  it. The tone is elegiac, not dramatic. It ends with the pilots who flew the last run knowing
  that they knew.
- Purpose: This fiction viewer does not depict the mission — it depicts what comes after,
  retrospectively. It tells the player, before they fly the mission, that the mission succeeds
  (the survivors get out) but Vega is lost permanently. The player knows the ending. The
  emotional work of the mission is therefore not "will they make it" but "how much do we take
  with us." This reframes the objectives as meaningful rather than merely tactical.

ENVIRONMENT AND SETUP
- Datestamp: January 24, 2335
- System: Vega — Antares node approach, outer system
- Location: 12,000m from the Vega-Antares jump node
- Background: The same corridor from Mission 1-3 but in the opposite direction. Vega's star is
  now a receding point astern, visually smaller than in earlier missions — the convoy is in the
  outer system. The Antares node shimmers directly ahead at medium range. It looks close.
  It is not close enough.
- Scene objects at mission start:
  - SC Malgor (SC Cain-class): Stationary at the node, 5,000m from node mouth. Blocking.
    Her turrets fire on anything that approaches. She is the cork in the bottle.
  - GTC Stentor: In scene, leading the convoy column. Hull: variable (carries over from Mission
    2-1 narrative — stated in CB not as a mechanic but as a dramatic note). Approximately 55%
    hull at mission start. She is damaged but mobile. She will attempt to transit to Antares.
    Stentor is the convoy's spine. If she falls, the convoy loses its escort capability.
  - Krishna wing: SF Scorpion x4 — already in scene, 4 km to port of the node. Fighter screen.
    Initial orders: Attack approaching convoy ships.
  - Indra wing: SF Basilisk x3 — in scene, 6 km ahead of convoy at node approach. Intercept.
    Initial orders: Attack GTC Stentor.
  - Arjuna wing: SF Dragon x2 — in scene at long range (10 km, toward node), patrolling.
    Initial orders: Intercept anything that approaches the node within 2 km. Elite blocking.
  - GTFr Aldrin 1, 2, 3 (if they survived Mission 2-1): In convoy column, behind Stentor.
    (Mission 2-1 survivability carries over narratively — noted in CB 2.2, not as a variable
    mechanic. For mission design purposes: 3 Chronos freighters present. If mission 2-1 partial
    result, acknowledgment in CB but full convoy deployed regardless for playability.)
  - GTT Rescue (if survived): Present in convoy.
  - PVT Theta 3 (if survived): Present in convoy — the last Vasudan transport, at rear.
  - Theta 1 and 2 are NOT present — they jumped to Antares independently in Mission 1-3 and
    are waiting safely at the Antares side.
  - GTS Casca: In convoy, positioned near Stentor.
  - Nav Alpha: Midpoint between convoy start and node (6,000m from node). Waypoint for convoy.
  - The GTEP Hermes pods from Mission 2-2 are now aboard the Stentor — they are not separate
    objects in this mission, but their presence is explicitly referenced in the briefing.

PARTICIPANTS

Allied:
- Alpha wing: GTF Valkyrie x4 (Alpha 1 = player, Alpha 2–4 = AI)
  - Loadout: GTW-15 Avenger (bank 1), GTW ML-16 (bank 2); GTM MX-50 (24), GTM Fury (16)
  - Initial orders: Escort GTC Stentor to the Antares node; intercept hostile fighters;
    destroy SC Malgor if possible to clear node approach
  - Note: Player is encouraged to try to destroy SC Malgor (or at least knock out key
    subsystems) before Stentor reaches the node. This is the secondary objective.

- GTC Stentor (GTC Fenris-class): Leading the convoy. Damaged but fighting.
  - Commander Okafor persona: she has been in this system for the entire campaign, she is tired,
    she knows the score, and she is going to get her ship and its passengers through that node.
  - Stentor will attempt to reach the node and transit, regardless of whether SC Malgor is
    destroyed. She takes fire while transiting. If her hull drops to 0% before she can transit,
    mission fails. (Stentor hull must survive to get to the node and initiate jump.)
  - Stentor's own turrets engage Krishna and Indra wings if they come within range.
  - Note: Stentor carries the survivors from the Amadeus escape pods. Stated explicitly in
    briefing. Commander Okafor: "The people from the Amadeus pods are aboard. They're going
    through that node."

- GTFr Aldrin 1, 2, 3 (GTFr Chronos-class): Following Stentor in column.
  - Each can jump to Antares independently once within 2,000m of the node — they don't need
    Stentor to already be through. They jump one by one.
  - Under attack from Krishna wing if player doesn't intercept.

- GTT Rescue (GTT Elysium-class): Following freighters in column.
  - Unarmed. Slowest in column. Last to reach node. Most vulnerable to delayed attacks.

- PVT Theta 3 (PVT Isis-class): At rear of convoy.
  - Has 3 turrets — fires at fighters within range. She's not helpless.
  - Slowest ship in the convoy. Arjuna (Dragons) may reach her first.

- GTS Casca: In convoy. Available for rearming.

Hostile:
- Krishna wing: SF Scorpion x4
  - In scene at mission start. 4 km to port. Targeting convoy ships (secondary priority:
    freighters and transports).
  - Initial orders: Attack GTFr Aldrin wing, Attack GTT Rescue
  - These are the harassment fighters. They try to slow the convoy by attacking the soft targets.

- Indra wing: SF Basilisk x3
  - In scene at mission start. 6 km ahead. Targeting Stentor.
  - Initial orders: Attack GTC Stentor
  - The Basilisks want to kill the convoy's spine. Intercepting them is a primary priority.

- SC Malgor (SC Cain-class): At the node. Blocking.
  - Turrets fire on any ship approaching within 6,000m of the node.
  - Malgor's cluster bomb fires if anything closes within 400m.
  - The Malgor is a mission objective — destroying her is the secondary objective. Player can
    engage Malgor's turrets (to reduce incoming fire on Stentor during transit) and then
    attack her hull. Without bombs, this takes sustained fire but IS possible with fighters if
    the player is methodical.
  - Key vulnerability: Malgor's weapons subsystem can be degraded by sustained Avenger fire on
    her turrets. Destroying the weapons subsystem reduces her incoming fire on the Stentor
    dramatically — a meaningful reward for targeting the right subsystem.
  - Malgor does NOT need to be destroyed for mission success. She needs to be suppressed enough
    that Stentor survives the node transit.

- Arjuna wing: SF Dragon x2
  - In scene at long range (10 km). Patrolling near the node.
  - Initial orders: When any ship comes within 2,000m of the node, attack it.
  - The Dragons activate when the convoy gets close. At that point the player is likely also
    engaging Malgor. Two converging threats at the moment of maximum vulnerability is the
    final test.
  - Only 2 Dragons (not 3 like Bheema wing in 2-2) — the campaign ends here, the challenge
    is real but the situation is not hopeless.

PLAYER CRAFT AND LOADOUT
- Ship: GTF Valkyrie (recommended; need speed for Dragon intercept near node)
- Primary: GTW-15 Avenger (bank 1), GTW ML-16 (bank 2)
- Secondary: GTM MX-50 (24), GTM Fury (16)
- Support: GTS Casca (always available)
- No reinforcements. No allied capital ships except Stentor.
- Design note: The player should use Casca to rearm before committing to the SC Malgor attack.
  Fighting Malgor + Arjuna wing simultaneously on depleted secondaries is the intended
  high-difficulty challenge.

OBJECTIVES AND DIRECTIVES

Primary:
  - GTC Stentor transits the Antares jump node.

Secondary:
  - SC Malgor destroyed (clears the node and removes the transit threat entirely).
  - At least 3 of the remaining convoy ships (excluding Stentor) transit the Antares node.

Bonus:
  - All surviving convoy ships transit the Antares node. (No one left behind.)
  - PVT Theta 3 specifically transits. (The last Vasudan transport — symbolic final beat for
    the Terran-Vasudan battlefield cooperation arc of Act 1.)

Failure conditions:
  - GTC Stentor destroyed before she can transit.
  - (Alpha 1 destroyed = standard.)

MISSION BRIEFING
[ Delivered by Commander Yael Okafor / GTC Stentor — for the first time this campaign ]

Stage 1:
This is Commander Okafor. Admiral Wei is gone, so I'm running this brief.
The Antares node is twelve thousand meters ahead. SC Malgor is sitting on it. She's been there
for four days. She has a fighter screen — Scorpions and Basilisks at the approach, and our
long-range sensors are showing Dragon-class contacts near the node itself.
We are going through that node. I need to be absolutely clear about that. There is no
alternative. The Amadeus is destroyed. The system is lost. We go through the node.

Stage 2:
The people from the Amadeus evacuation pods are aboard the Stentor. Nine pods, most of them
intact. They are in the cargo bays right now. I will not tell you what it means to me personally
to have them aboard. I will tell you that they are going through that node if I have to take
the Stentor through the Malgor on a collision course.
Alpha wing, your job is to suppress the Malgor and clear the fighter screen. Get her turrets
down if you can. If you can take out her weapons subsystem, do it — that's what matters. Destroy
her if you have time.
We move on my mark. Do not hesitate when the node is clear enough. Transit and don't look back.

Stage 3:
One more thing. There's a Vasudan transport at the back of the convoy. PVT Theta Three.
She was here when no one asked her to be. She goes through that node. That is not optional.
Okafor out.

EVENT FLOW AND TIMING
- T+00:00: Mission starts. Convoy stationary, 12 km from node. SC Malgor at node. Krishna
  wing (4 Scorpions) at port, 4 km. Indra wing (3 Basilisks) ahead, 6 km. Arjuna (2 Dragons)
  near node, 10 km, in patrol mode.
- T+00:00 onward: Convoy begins advancing toward node. Krishna and Indra engage convoy/Stentor.
  Player must intercept to protect convoy while convoy advances.
- T+00:00–T+05:00: Player clears Krishna and Indra wings while convoy approaches. SC Malgor
  begins firing on anything within 6 km once convoy reaches that range.
- T+03:00–T+05:00 (convoy reaches 6 km from node): SC Malgor opens fire on Stentor.
  Player must engage Malgor's turrets / weapons subsystem.
- T+05:00–T+08:00: Convoy reaches Nav Alpha (6 km from node). Arjuna (Dragons) activates —
  begins intercepting ships approaching within 2 km of node.
- T+06:00 onward: Stentor reaches transit range (2 km from node) and begins transit sequence.
  Malgor fires if weapons subsystem not yet destroyed. Arjuna intercepts.
  Player must simultaneously fight Dragons, suppress Malgor if active, protect trailing ships.
- T+06:00–T+12:00: Ships transit in sequence: Stentor first, then freighters, then Rescue,
  then Theta 3. Each ship that reaches within 2 km of node begins an 8-second transit sequence.
  Ships in transit sequence can still be killed (hull goes to 0% before transit completes =
  dead). This is the maximum tension window.
- When all ships that will transit have done so: Alpha wing is the last to go. Mission ends
  when Alpha 1 transits the node.

EVENT LOGIC SKETCH
- "Krishna wing engage convoy" and "Indra wing engage Stentor": set at mission start, active
  immediately. Both wings advance toward convoy from their positions.
- "SC Malgor fire on approaching ships": Malgor AI turret range set to 6,000m. When Stentor
  enters this range, Malgor's turrets engage. Cannot be disabled except by weapons subsystem
  destruction or Malgor's destruction.
- "Malgor weapons subsystem degraded": when weapons subsystem hull < 20%, all turret fire from
  Malgor is suppressed.
- "Arjuna wing activates": when any convoy ship (or Alpha 1) comes within 2,000m of the node.
  Arjuna changes AI goal from patrol to "attack all approaching ships." One-time trigger.
- "Ship transit sequence": any ship within 2,000m of the node that is not under active fire
  (or is willing to transit under fire) initiates an 8-second jump count. If hull reaches 0%
  during this count, the ship is destroyed (fails to transit). Otherwise: transit complete.
  Stentor initiates this. Freighters follow when Stentor's transit is confirmed. Rescue follows
  freighters. Theta 3 follows Rescue (she's slowest, arrives last).
- "Mission success" condition: GTC Stentor has "transit complete" flag set AND Alpha 1 has
  "transit complete" flag set (player jumps through node).
- "Mission failure" condition: GTC Stentor hull = 0% at any point before transit complete flag.
- Secondary objective tracked: SC Malgor "destroyed" flag. Convoy "transit complete" count >= 3.
- Bonus objective: All convoy ships transit (count = all present ships). Theta 3 transit flag.
- GTS Casca: if Alpha 1 is near Casca and requests rearm, Casca executes rearm. Casca transits
  the node immediately after Stentor (she's a small ship — she goes second).

NEWLY INTRODUCED SHIPS AND WEAPONS
- No new ship classes. This mission is a test of everything the player has learned.
- The presence of SF Dragons (Arjuna wing) at the node is thematically important: the Shivans
  are deploying their best fighters to hold their most important position. The Vega system's
  final node is guarded by Dragons. It is a correct and terrifying choice.
- SC Malgor callback: she was first introduced in Mission 1-2 as a threat the player could not
  engage. Now, with Avengers and full situational understanding, the player can fight her. This
  is the arc of the entire campaign compressed into one target.

DEBRIEFINGS

Full success (Stentor through, Malgor destroyed, all convoy ships through, Theta 3 through):
All ships are through the node. Every person who was alive in the Vega system when we started
this transit is alive now in Antares.
The SC Malgor is destroyed.
Commander Okafor has submitted her after-action report. I will summarize it here: "The convoy
executed the transit under sustained Shivan fire. The conduct of all crews, and of Alpha wing
in particular, was exemplary. I commend Alpha wing to the attention of Terran Command for
recognition."
Admiral Wei would have said something better than I can. I'm going to leave space here for
what he would have said and let you fill it in yourself.
Welcome to Antares. The war isn't over. But everyone who should have made it did.

Primary success (Stentor through, 3+ convoy ships through):
The convoy is through. The Stentor is safe. The people from the Amadeus pods are in Antares.
We lost [count] ships on the transit. Those were ships with crews and cargo — things that
can be mourned and then accounted for, which is the GTA's way of handling loss.
[If Theta 3 through]: The Vasudan transport Theta Three made the transit. Her pilot asked me
to pass along a message. I'll put it in the report.
[If Theta 3 lost]: We lost PVT Theta Three on the transit. She made it this far. That counts
for something.
The Vega system is behind us now. It isn't coming back.

Partial success (Stentor through, fewer than 3 convoy ships through):
The Stentor is through. That's what matters. The people from the Amadeus pods are alive.
We lost most of the convoy on the transit. The Malgor, the Dragons at the node — they were
too much, and we were too few, and the math didn't work out.
Commander Okafor says this: "The node is closed behind us. We grieve what we left and we carry
what we saved."
I don't have a better summary of this operation than that.

Failure (Stentor destroyed before transit):
The Stentor did not make it through the node.
The people from the Amadeus pods — the crew that Admiral Wei stayed behind to protect — are
gone. The SC Malgor, or the Dragons, or the accumulated damage that the Stentor carried from
two weeks of fighting — one of those things ended it.
Commander Okafor's last recorded transmission: "Convoy, scatter. Every ship independent.
Make for the node on your own. Don't wait for us."
Some did. Some didn't. The accounting will take time.
The Vega system is closed.

QA NOTES
- This mission is the campaign finale. The ending dialogue and debrief text need to land
  cleanly. Ensure no in-mission messages overlap with Theta 3's final transmission (Message 13)
  — this is the Terran-Vasudan arc's payoff and must play without interruption.
- GTC Stentor must survive to transit. Her hull carrying over from Mission 2-1 is a narrative
  note, not a mechanical difficulty modifier — her in-mission starting hull should be fixed at
  ~55% regardless of Mission 2-1 outcome, to keep the finale playable.
- SC Malgor's turret-range threat at 6,000m creates a natural pacing gate: the convoy can
  advance freely until 6 km from the node, then the final engagement begins. This ensures
  the Krishna/Indra fight has resolved (mostly) before the Malgor engagement starts.
- Arjuna (Dragon) activation within 2,000m of node: this is the last threat and must feel
  like a final gate rather than a trivial speed bump. Two Dragons near the node while the
  player is also managing Malgor fire is the intended difficulty spike.
- "Player jumps last" design: the mission should not end until Alpha 1 transits the node.
  After all convoy ships have jumped, a grace period of 30 seconds allows the player to
  finish any Dragon/Malgor engagement before jumping. After 30 seconds, Command requests
  the player transit ("Alpha Lead, the node is clear — jump when ready").
- If the player is still fighting Arjuna wing when all convoy ships have transited, they can
  choose to kill the Dragons before jumping or simply jump and let them be. Either is valid.
  The campaign does not require a kill count; it requires getting people out.
- Casca transit: Casca goes through the node after Stentor, ahead of the freighters. She
  does not need player escort — her small hull and high speed get her through quickly. She
  should not require the player's attention.
