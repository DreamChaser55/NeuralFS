================================================================================
MISSION DESIGN DOCUMENT
Mission 2-2: "The Last Full Measure"
================================================================================

PURPOSE AND PLAYER EXPERIENCE
- Mission fantasy: The player is the only thing standing between nine escape pods and everything
  that wants to destroy them. The Amadeus is burning behind them. The Admiral is still aboard.
  The player cannot save the ship. They can only save the people.
- Core tension: Nine pods. Three waves of enemies. A Lilith-class cruiser that is now closing
  to finish the Amadeus. Bheema wing — SF Dragon fighters, the most dangerous Shivan craft the
  player has encountered — arrives at the worst possible moment. The player has to keep moving,
  keep fighting, keep the pods alive while watching the Amadeus die.
- Plot advance: GTD Amadeus destroyed. Admiral Wei dead. The campaign's central command
  structure collapses. What remains is the GTC Stentor, four freighters, two Vasudan transports,
  and Alpha wing — and they need to get to the Antares node.
- Emotional register: This is the campaign's funeral. It should feel weightless and terrible.
  The Admiral's calm voice in the first minutes. The silence when he goes. The long run to Nav
  Bravo with the pods drifting and the fires behind you.
- Design note: The player cannot save the Amadeus. They cannot save Admiral Wei. This must be
  designed so that no amount of skill or preparation allows a different outcome for the ship.
  The drama is in the pods — variable, dependent on the player, meaningful.

FICTION VIEWER NARRATIVE
- Displayed before this mission.
- File: Mission_2_2_story.txt
- Summary: Admiral Chen Wei's personal log the night before. He explains, clearly and without
  self-pity, why he is staying aboard. He is not performing sacrifice — he is doing his job
  with the tools he has available, and he is at peace with it.
- Purpose: The player knows what is coming. They read the Admiral's words and then they fly
  the mission knowing those words were the last thing he wrote. The fiction viewer converts
  the mission's scripted tragedy into something personal and earned.

ENVIRONMENT AND SETUP
- Datestamp: January 22, 2335
- System: Vega — deep space, Nav Charlie holding position (approximately 12,000m from the
  Antares node approach corridor, 7,000m from Nav Bravo)
- Location: GTD Amadeus is stationary. She cannot move — she stopped short of Nav Bravo when
  her drive failed en route from Mission 2-1. Her aft section shows significant structural
  damage (visible hull deformation, secondary explosions, fires burning in the vacuum where
  internal atmosphere vents through breaches). She is holding position on the Antares approach
  axis, 7,000m from Nav Bravo and approximately 12,000m from the Antares node approach corridor,
  facing the incoming threat direction.
- Background: The scene should feel like the inside of a catastrophe. The Amadeus is large and
  damaged and beautiful and dying. Nav Charlie is a point in space with no other features.
  Deep space, Vega star far astern, the Antares node shimmer barely visible at extreme range.
- Objects in scene at mission start:
  - GTD Amadeus: Stationary. Hull at 40%. Her turrets are operational — she fires at Shivans
    within range until she can no longer. Her fighterbay is open: a visible cavity in her flank
    from which the escape pods emerge in sequence.
  - Nine GTEP Hermes escape pods: Emerging from the Amadeus fighterbay at mission start, in
    groups of three. They navigate autonomously toward Nav Bravo (7,000m distant).
    Numbering convention: Pod 1 through Pod 9. Pod 1 is designated "Admiral's Pod" — Wei's
    escape pod, the one he theoretically would have boarded. (He didn't. It launched with only
    bridge crew.) Pod 1 carries the most senior surviving officers.
  - Nav Charlie: The Amadeus's holding position. No navbuoy — just the designation.
  - Nav Bravo: 7,000m from Amadeus position. The pods' destination.
  - SC Ahriman (SC Lilith-class): 4,000m from Amadeus, closing slowly. She begins firing effectively on the
    Amadeus as she reaches ~2,500m. She's the ship's executioner. Her weapons do capital-level damage. The
    Amadeus's turrets fire back but cannot stop her.
  - GTS Casca: Present, following Pod 1 group. Available for rearming. Casca's presence
    near the pods makes her somewhat protected by proximity — the enemy is targeting pods, not
    a support ship, so Casca survives unless the player is extremely unlucky.
  - No Beta wing. No Stentor. They are ahead, already at Nav Bravo, waiting. Alone.
  - No installations, no buoys, no cover. Just the Amadeus and nine pods and the dark.

PARTICIPANTS

Allied:
- Alpha wing: GTF Valkyrie x4 (Alpha 1 = player, Alpha 2–4 = AI)
  - Loadout: GTW-15 Avenger (bank 1), GTW ML-16 (bank 2); GTM MX-50 (24), GTM Fury (16)
  - Initial orders: Protect escape pods (priority ordered: Pod 1 highest, Pod 9 lowest,
    but the player can and should protect whichever is most threatened)

- Pod 1 through Pod 9 (GTEP Hermes x9): Mission's primary protected assets
  - All nine pods present at mission start, emerging from Amadeus in three groups over
    the first 2 minutes (Group A: Pods 1–3 emerge at T+00:00; Group B: Pods 4–6 emerge at
    T+00:45; Group C: Pods 7–9 emerge at T+01:30)
  - All navigate toward Nav Bravo at slow pod speed. No weapons. Minimal hull.
  - Pod 1 is explicitly labeled "ADMIRAL'S POD" in the targeting system. (Wei's voice notes
    this at one point — "Pod One carries the bridge officers. Keep it clear." He does not
    say he's not on it. He doesn't need to. The player can see the fighterbay is still closed
    on the Admiral's section.)
  - Pods reaching Nav Bravo are considered "safe" and no longer counted as vulnerable.

- TAC-1 Tech Cargo Container and GTFr Aldrin 4 (GTFr Chronos-class):
  - GTFr Aldrin 4 emerges from the Amadeus alongside Pod Group B, carrying the TAC-1 container clamped to its hull. Contains the ship's encrypted tactical databanks and Avenger prototype schematics.
  - Aldrin 4 is slow, heavily targeted by the Shivans, and completely unarmed. The player must make a wrenching tactical choice: protecting this freighter likely means sacrificing at least one escape pod due to split attention. Command acknowledges the choice in the debriefing.

- GTD Amadeus (GTD Orion-class): Stationary, dying
  - Hull: 40% at start. Ahriman is closing in. She will be destroyed before mission end —
    this is scripted. Wei's voice continues until his last transmission ("All pods are away.
    Casca, report to Nav Bravo. Alpha wing — you have your orders."). Then silence.
  - Amadeus turrets fire at nearby Shivans until the ship's weapons subsystem fails (or
    hull reaches 0%). The turrets buying time for the pods is a tangible mechanical benefit.
  - The Amadeus's explosion when she dies is the largest in the campaign — a capital ship
    detonation visible for kilometers. The blast has a shockwave radius that will damage
    fighters within 1,000m. Warning issued. Player must clear the area before final destruction.

- GTS Casca (GTS Centaur-class): Present, following pod group A
  - Available for rearming throughout the mission.
  - Casca has enhanced narrative importance here: she is the only allied capital-class object
    that can survive this mission.

- Admiral Wei (radio persona only, aboard Amadeus): Present as a voice for the first portion
  of the mission. His last transmission fires when the Amadeus hull drops below 5%.
  After that: silence.

Hostile:
- Asura wing: SF Scorpion x3
  - Present at mission start, 6 km from pod group A. Already attacking.
  - Initial orders: Attack escape pods (explicit — they are targeting the pods, not Alpha wing)
  - This is the first time in the campaign that enemy fighters explicitly prioritize pods over
    the player's wing. It is immediately apparent that these enemies must be intercepted, not
    chased.

- Brahma wing: SB Shaitan x3 (Amadeus killers)
  - Arrives at T+01:30 from the direction of the Capella approach (9 km)
  - Initial orders: Attack GTD Amadeus (primary, to complete her destruction)
  - The Shaitans target the Amadeus, not the pods. This creates a agonizing choice: intercept
    the Shaitans and maybe reduce damage to the doomed ship (buying time, slightly), or focus
    on Asura wing which is actively destroying pods. The correct answer changes second by second.
  - Note: Regardless of player action, the Amadeus will be destroyed. Brahma wing's attack
    accelerates it but doesn't cause it — Ahriman is doing that anyway. Intercepting Brahma
    is genuinely optional from a mission success standpoint.

- Vishnu wing: SF Scorpion x4
  - Arrives at T+03:00 from the aft direction (12 km)
  - Initial orders: Attack escape pods (pod group B and C specifically — groups that are
    emerging or have just emerged from the Amadeus fighterbay)
  - Second pod-hunting wave. More Scorpions means faster kills. Player must have dealt with
    Asura significantly before this arrives.

- Bheema wing: SF Dragon x3
  - Arrives at T+06:00 or when 5 pods have reached Nav Bravo (whichever comes first)
  - Initial orders: Attack escape pods; Attack Alpha wing
  - The Dragon is the most maneuverable, most dangerous Shivan fighter in the catalog. Three of
    them targeting the remaining pods at this point is the mission's final and hardest test.
  - Dragons have strong shields and exceptional maneuverability. They are not helpless against
    Avenger fire, but they take more sustained fire than Scorpions.
  - The player who has been fighting effectively for 6 minutes may be running low on secondaries.
    Casca rearming before this wave is strongly advisable.

- SC Ahriman (SC Lilith-class): Closes from 8 km to point-blank on the Amadeus
  - Not targetable by Alpha wing as a mission objective. She does not fire on pods or fighters
    unless a fighter comes within 400m (cluster bomb).
  - Ahriman's job: kill the Amadeus. She does it systematically and without haste.
  - When the Amadeus hull drops below 5%, the final Admiral transmission fires.
  - Ahriman withdraws after Amadeus is destroyed (she has completed her mission).

PLAYER CRAFT AND LOADOUT
- Ship: GTF Valkyrie (only recommendation — Dragon intercept requires speed)
- Primary: GTW-15 Avenger (bank 1), GTW ML-16 (bank 2)
- Secondary: GTM MX-50 (24), GTM Fury (16)
- Support: GTS Casca (always available — critical to rearm before Bheema)
- Reinforcements: None. Alpha is alone.
- Design note: This is the hardest mission in the campaign. No reinforcements, no allied capital
  ships in the immediate scene, nine fragile pods, three waves of enemies, and Dragons at the end.
  The player is expected to use Casca for rearm, to prioritize effectively, and to accept that
  some pods may be lost while others are saved.

OBJECTIVES AND DIRECTIVES

Primary:
  - At least 6 of 9 escape pods reach Nav Bravo.

Secondary:
  - At least 8 of 9 pods reach Nav Bravo. (Near-complete evacuation.)
  - Save GTFr Aldrin 4 (carrying the TAC-1 Tech Cargo). (Forces a moral choice: data vs. lives).

Bonus:
  - All 9 pods reach Nav Bravo. (Perfect evacuation — extremely difficult; requires killing
    Asura wing before any pod takes a fatal hit AND managing the Dragon wave tightly.)
  - Pod 1 (Admiral's Pod / bridge officers) specifically reaches Nav Bravo. (Separate flag.)

Failure conditions:
  - Fewer than 6 pods reach Nav Bravo.
  - (Alpha 1 destroyed = standard.)

BRIEFINGS (TEXT-READY)

[ No Command Briefing before this mission — the situation has been communicated directly by
  Admiral Wei. The player proceeds from Mission 2-1 debrief directly to the fiction viewer
  (Admiral's log) and then into the mission. ]

MISSION BRIEFING
[ Delivered by Admiral Wei himself — a unique briefing voice. Calm. Final. ]

Stage 1:
This is Admiral Chen Wei. I'll be brief because the situation is simple.
The Amadeus cannot transit to Antares. Her subspace drive is non-functional. We've run the
numbers. We're holding here at Nav Charlie.
The crew is being evacuated in nine Hermes pods. They'll move to Nav Bravo and continue to the
Antares node under Stentor's escort. All non-essential personnel are clearing the ship now.

Stage 2:
Alpha wing, your assignment is the pods. Nine of them, three groups. They come out in sequence
over the next two minutes. You get them to Nav Bravo.
The Ahriman is closing. We'll use the Amadeus's position and turrets to cover the pod corridor
for as long as we can.
I would appreciate it if you did not waste time trying to protect this ship. The Amadeus has
served her purpose. The people in those pods have not.

Stage 3:
One final item. I am aware that some of you know what this briefing implies about where I will
be during this operation. I want it noted for the log that I consider this the correct decision
and that I made it freely. Command will review the documentation.
Good luck. Amadeus out.

[ Briefing icon notes: Amadeus position at Nav Charlie marked. Nine pod positions marked with
  alpha-numeric labels. Nav Bravo marked as destination. SC Ahriman position marked on approach
  vector — explicitly labeled "NOT A TARGET." Asura wing entry position marked at 6 km. Brahma,
  Vishnu, Bheema entry vectors marked as estimated threat sectors with time estimates. GTS Casca
  marked near Pod Group A. ]

IN-MISSION COMMUNICATIONS

Message 1 — Admiral Wei / Amadeus, trigger: mission start
"Alpha wing, pods are away. Group A has cleared the bay. Watch your left — we're showing
contacts at six kilometers."

Message 2 — Alpha 2, persona: veteran wingman, trigger: Asura wing first fires on a pod
"They're going for the pods directly. Forget the Amadeus — these things are targeting the pods."

Message 3 — Admiral Wei, trigger: Pod 1 (Group A lead) clears 5,000m from Amadeus
"Pod One is clear. Bridge officers are aboard. Get them to Nav Bravo."

Message 4 — Alpha 3, trigger: Brahma wing (Shaitans) arrival (T+01:30)
"Bombers inbound — they're going for the Amadeus. Do we— Command?"

Message 5 — Command / Amadeus CAG (different from Wei's channel), trigger: Brahma wing arrival
"Alpha wing, the Amadeus is not your objective. Let the Amadeus handle the Shaitans. Pods.
Focus on the pods."

Message 6 — Admiral Wei, trigger: Amadeus hull below 60% (from Ahriman fire)
"Some damage to frame forty. Manageable. Turrets holding. Alpha Lead, how are the pods?"

Message 7 — Alpha 2, trigger: Vishnu wing arrival (T+03:00)
"Another fighter wave — Scorpions, coming out of the aft sector. Four of them."

Message 8 — Alpha 2, trigger: 5 pods have reached Nav Bravo
"Five pods at Nav Bravo. Halfway there. Keep pushing."

Message 9 — Admiral Wei, trigger: Amadeus hull below 20%
"Significant structural compromise, frames twenty through sixty. Casca — you'll want to move
away from the ship." [pause] "Alpha Lead. Don't mourn on my clock. Not yet."

Message 10 — Alpha 3 or Alpha 4 (whichever still alive), trigger: Bheema wing (Dragons) arrival
"Dragons. Three of them. They're fast — they're going for the pods."

Message 11 — Admiral Wei (FINAL TRANSMISSION), trigger: Amadeus hull below 5%
"All pods are away. All pods are clear." [A pause. The sound of secondary explosions in the
background.] "Alpha wing — you have your orders. Amadeus out."
[Then: static. His channel goes silent. He does not transmit again.]

Message 12 — Alpha 2 (after a 15-second silence following Wei's last message), trigger:
fired 15 seconds after message 11
"...Copy, Admiral."
[Nothing else.]

Message 13 — Commander Okafor / GTC Stentor (from Nav Bravo), trigger: Amadeus destruction
"Alpha Lead, this is Stentor at Nav Bravo. The Amadeus — we're reading... confirmed destroyed.
Get those pods to us. Bring everyone you can."

Message 14 — Command, trigger: 7+ pods reach Nav Bravo
"Seven pods at Nav Bravo. Alpha Lead, disengage and return when the area is clear."

Message 15 — Command, trigger: all remaining pods at Nav Bravo / mission end condition met
"Pods are safe. Alpha wing, withdraw to Nav Bravo. We're done here."

EVENT FLOW AND TIMING
- T+00:00: Mission starts. Alpha wing at mid-position between Amadeus and Nav Bravo.
  Pod Group A (Pods 1–3) emerging from fighterbay, navigating to Nav Bravo (7 km away).
  Asura wing (3 Scorpions) already at 6 km, converging on pods.
  SC Ahriman at 4,000m, closing on Amadeus.
- T+00:00–T+01:00: Asura wing engages pod group A. Player must intercept. Ahriman begins
  firing effectively on Amadeus as she reaches ~2,500m.
- T+00:45: Pod Group B (Pods 4–6) emerges from fighterbay.
- T+01:30: Pod Group C (Pods 7–9) emerges from fighterbay.
  Brahma wing (3 Shaitans) arrives from Capella direction, 9 km. Targets Amadeus.
- T+03:00: Vishnu wing (4 Scorpions) arrives from aft, 10 km. Targets pods (Group B and C).
- T+03:00–T+06:00: Sustained engagement. Asura, Vishnu, possibly remnants of Brahma. Player
  should rearm from Casca in this window.
- T+06:00 (or when 5 pods are at Nav Bravo, whichever first): Bheema wing (3 Dragons) arrives.
  Targets pods. Dragon fight is the final and hardest wave.
  Note: Pod Group A (7 km at ~20 m/s) begins reaching Nav Bravo around T+05:50, so the
  5-pod trigger is likely to fire around T+06:00 — both conditions resolve near-simultaneously.
- Amadeus hull track: Declining through Ahriman fire + Brahma attack. Reaches 5% at approximately
  T+07:00–T+09:00 (adjusted for how much damage Brahma deals — Dragon intercept may slow them).
- T+07:00–T+09:00: Admiral Wei's final transmission fires at 5% hull. Amadeus destroyed shortly
  after (2–3 seconds). Large explosion. Shockwave at 1,000m radius.
  Player WARNING message fires 30 seconds before Amadeus hull reaches 5%: "Amadeus structural
  failure imminent — clear the ship's immediate area."
- T+06:00–T+10:00: Remaining pods reach Nav Bravo. Bheema wing remaining ships continue
  pursuit. Mission ends when 6+ pods are at Nav Bravo AND no remaining enemy ships are targeting
  unprotected pods in transit (or when all surviving pods have reached Nav Bravo).

EVENT LOGIC SKETCH
- "Pod Group A emerges" at mission start (T+00:00). Group B at T+00:45. Group C at T+01:30.
- "Brahma wing arrival" fires at T+01:30 unconditionally.
- "Vishnu wing arrival" fires at T+03:00 unconditionally.
- "Bheema wing arrival" fires at T+06:00 OR when 5 pods have their "at Nav Bravo" flag set,
  whichever comes first.
- "Amadeus hull at 5%" event: fires Admiral's final transmission, then begins 10-second count
  to Amadeus destruction. Shockwave applied to ships within 1,000m.
- "Mission success" condition: Pod "at Nav Bravo" count >= 6.
- "Mission failure" condition: Pod destroyed count > 3 (fewer than 6 surviving pods) AND all
  surviving pods are at Nav Bravo (i.e., no recovery possible). 
  Actually simplified: mission fails if destroyed pod count reaches 4 at any point, since
  maximum possible safe pods then drops below 6.
- "Pod 1 bonus" condition: Pod 1 "at Nav Bravo" flag set.
- Casca: follows Pod Group A, available for rearming on demand throughout.
- Ahriman: AI goal "attack GTD Amadeus" throughout. Does not fire on fighters unless they
  come within 400m (cluster bomb trigger). Withdraws after Amadeus destroyed.
- Asura, Vishnu, Bheema: AI goal "attack escape pods" (target by name). They will pursue
  pods rather than engaging Alpha wing unless Alpha wing fires on them. This creates the
  interception dynamic — player must go to them, not wait for them to come to Alpha.

NEWLY INTRODUCED SHIPS AND WEAPONS
- SF Dragon (Bheema wing): First concentrated Dragon engagement in the campaign. The Dragon
  has been referenced in earlier briefings as the Shivans' most capable fighter. Meeting three
  of them while protecting fragile pods is the difficulty peak of the entire campaign.
  Their maneuverability must be communicated in the debrief: players who struggled should know
  what they were up against.
- GTEP Hermes in large numbers (9 pods): The player has seen pods before (Mission 1-1). Now
  there are nine, and the Admiral is nominally on one. The emotional weight of protecting them
  is entirely built on the preceding fiction viewer.

DEBRIEFINGS

Full bonus success (all 9 pods survived, Pod 1 survived):
All nine escape pods reached Nav Bravo. The entire crew of the Amadeus is accounted for.
Admiral Chen Wei is listed as killed in action. He remained aboard as a decision he made
openly and documented. His service record will reflect that.
Every person in those pods owes their life to Alpha wing. I don't know another way to say that.
The Amadeus is gone. She served for eleven years and she died doing what a ship is supposed to
do — buying time for people who needed it.
Brace yourself. We still have to get through the Antares node. And the SC Malgor is sitting
on it.

Primary success (6–8 pods survived):
[Number] of nine escape pods reached Nav Bravo. We lost [number] in the evacuation.
Admiral Wei is listed as killed in action. His log and his decisions have been forwarded to
Terran Command.
You held the line against three fighter waves and a Dragon flight. Whatever we lost out there,
it could have been worse. Today's standard is survival, not perfection.
The Malgor is still at the Antares node. We move as soon as the Stentor can reposition.

Failure (fewer than 6 pods survived):
The evacuation failed. Of the nine pods that departed the Amadeus, only [number] made it.
Admiral Wei is listed as killed in action alongside the crew of the pods that were destroyed.
I don't have words for this that are useful right now. We proceed because we have to.
Whatever is at the Antares node, we have to go through it. All of us who are left.

QA NOTES
- The Admiral's final transmission is the most important single piece of audio in this campaign.
  It must be protected from overlap with any other message for ±15 seconds. The 15-second
  silence before Alpha 2's "...Copy, Admiral" is mandatory. Do not cut this.
- The explosion of the Amadeus must not instantly kill the player. The 30-second warning
  message and the "clear the area" instruction give the player time to move. The 1,000m
  shockwave radius is punishing but not lethal to a Valkyrie at 1,100m distance. Player
  who ignores the warning and stays close WILL take hull damage.
- Bheema wing (Dragons) must arrive when the player is already tired and potentially low on
  missiles, not at a point when they're fresh. The T+06:00 / 5-pods-safe trigger ensures this.
- Pods must move slowly enough that the player can intercept fighters before they complete
  an attack run on a pod, but not so slowly that the mission extends beyond ~15 minutes total.
  Calibrate pod speed relative to fighter attack run time carefully.
- SC Ahriman must NOT be attackable as a mission objective, but the player will be tempted.
  The cluster bomb at 400m and Command's redirect message handle this. Ahriman's withdrawal
  after Amadeus destruction prevents her from becoming a post-mission threat to pods.
- "Pod 1 is Admiral's pod" labeling should be visible in the targeting system and noted in
  the briefing. Players who protect it specifically should be rewarded in the debrief.
- Mission must not softlock if Bheema wing arrives and all remaining pods are at Nav Bravo
  simultaneously — in that case, Bheema should be marked as "objectives no longer reachable"
  and the mission end condition fires normally.
