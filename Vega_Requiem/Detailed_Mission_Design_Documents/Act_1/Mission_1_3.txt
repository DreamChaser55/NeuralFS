================================================================================
COMMAND BRIEFING 1.2
"The Enemy of My Enemy"
================================================================================

Delivered aboard: GTD Amadeus
Location: Vega System
Date: January 18, 2335

--- STAGE 1: SHIVANS CONFIRMED ---
Attention, pilots. This is Rear Admiral Chen Wei.

Terran Command has now officially confirmed what Alpha wing brought back from the Capella
corridor three days ago. The unidentified contacts are not Vasudan. They are not any species or
faction in the GTA database. Command has designated them "Shivans," pending further data. That
designation comes from their apparent intent, which is the destruction of everything that
encounters them.

The GTI Vega Prime installation was destroyed this morning. We hold this system by the barest
margins. The GTC Stentor is conducting running repairs. We lost four pilots in the last
forty-eight hours. Their names are on the board in the corridor outside the briefing room. I
expect you to read them.

--- STAGE 2: NEW WEAPONS ---
The GTW-15 Avenger cannon prototypes began arriving on the Amadeus this morning. R&D has modified
the Avenger specifically based on your sensor data — the weapon is calibrated to suppress Shivan
shield harmonics. It is not a miracle. But it is a significant improvement over the ML-16 against
their shields.

We have enough units for one field-test assignment right now. Additional units are expected within
three days. The pilot who draws the Avenger assignment today will be leading Alpha wing, and I
expect a full sensor report on its combat effectiveness.

--- STAGE 3: THE VASUDAN SITUATION ---
You will have questions about what I'm about to say. I ask that you hold them until the end.

We have received a priority signal from a Vasudan warship — the PVD Vigil, commanding a 9th Fleet
advance element — indicating they are transiting into Vega system from the Capella node. Their
stated mission: engage the Shivan presence in Vega. They report three refugee transports under
escort — Vasudan civilians from an outpost that was overrun.

The ceasefire negotiations are still ongoing. We are NOT at peace with the Vasudans. What we
are, today, in this system, is outgunned by something that is killing both of us with equal
commitment.

I have received no orders from Terran Command on how to handle Vasudan forces that are fighting
the same enemy we are. I am therefore operating on my own initiative. The Vega-Antares jump node
is open. If those refugee transports need to use it, I see no reason to prevent them.

I have not asked any of you to like the Vasudans. I'm asking you to stay focused on what matters.

--- STAGE 4: HAMMER OF LIGHT WARNING ---
One more element. Intelligence has flagged activity in Vega from a faction calling themselves
the Hammer of Light — a Vasudan splinter group that appears to be actively aiding the Shivans.
They have been reported attacking Vasudan assets. If you encounter ships broadcasting HoL
transponders, treat them as hostile. They have given us no reason to do otherwise.

Report to your wing stations in twenty minutes.

================================================================================
MISSION DESIGN DOCUMENT
Mission 1-3: "No Truce With the Tide"
================================================================================

PURPOSE AND PLAYER EXPERIENCE
- Mission fantasy: The player operates in a moral and tactical gray zone — protecting erstwhile
  enemies from a shared threat, adapting the new Avenger cannon in real combat, and watching
  the horror of the ambush unfold while doing the only productive thing available: saving the
  survivors.
- Core tension: Tripartite threat (Shivans + Hammer of Light), fragile VIP targets (Theta
  transports), and a capital ship the player cannot kill (PVD Vigil). The Vigil dies early and
  publicly, making the mission feel like a tragic rescue rather than a defense.
- Plot advance: The alliance between Terrans and Vasudans is not official — it begins here, in
  this mission, as a private choice made by one pilot. The Vasudan commander's brief transmission
  before going silent is the emotional payoff of the whole act.
- Avenger cannon introduction: The player FEELS the difference against shields. Shivans start
  dying at a meaningful rate. Hope enters, carefully.
- New enemies: Hammer of Light as an active battlefield threat for the first time. They're Vasudan
  equipment but HoL markings — the player has been shooting Vasudans for fourteen years and now
  must distinguish between the ones who are allies in fact and the ones who are enemies in faith.

FICTION VIEWER NARRATIVE
- Displayed before this mission.
- File: Mission_1_3_story.txt
- Summary: Told from inside the PVD Vigil. The navigation officer catalogs the approach to Vega,
  realizes the trap as it closes, and logs Sub-Imperator Hethor's final order: "Theta wing —
  make for the Antares node. Do not wait for us."
- Purpose: The Vigil dies almost immediately in-mission. Without this setup, her destruction
  would feel impersonal. With it, the player understands: her crew knew, her commander chose
  to die covering the transports, and the Vasudan people who are going to need the player's help
  in a moment were worth what she paid.

ENVIRONMENT AND SETUP
- Datestamp: January 20, 2335
- System: Vega — Capella node approach corridor, outer system
- Location: 8,000m from the Vega-Capella jump node
- Background: Deep space; Vega star small and bright astern; the Capella node visible as a
  stable subspace shimmer at close range. The system feels emptier than it should — no station
  now, just the void.
- Scene at mission start:
  - PVD Vigil (PVD Typhon-class): Emerging from the Capella node at T+00:20, already surrounded
    by converging Shivan contacts. She is the largest object in the scene and she is already dying.
  - Theta 1, 2, 3 (PVT Isis x3): Also emerging from the Capella node. Slow, unarmed, heading
    away from the Vigil and toward the Antares vector. They're following Hethor's last order.
  - Brahma wing (SF Scorpion x4): Already in scene, converging on Capella node from the flank —
    they've been waiting here.
  - Aquarius wing (PVF Horus x4, HoL): In scene on the far side of the node, moving to intercept
    Theta transports. Their HoL markings are visible and distinct from PVN standard.
  - Nav Alpha: The Vega-Antares node approach point, 45,000m from current position. The goal.
  - Two Terran NavBuoys marking the standard corridor to Nav Alpha.
  - No GTA capital ships in this area — the Amadeus is holding deeper in the system.
  - No sentry guns, no installations. Open space.
  - Scattered wreckage from the earlier station fight has drifted into this corridor: three
    destroyed GTSG Watchdog hulls, some TAC-1 containers (cannot be scanned — too damaged).

PARTICIPANTS

Allied (ad-hoc — no formal alliance):
- Alpha wing: GTF Valkyrie x4 (Alpha 1 = player, Alpha 2–4 = AI)
  - Alpha 1 loadout: GTW-15 Avenger x1, GTW ML-16 Laser x1 (Avenger in bank 1 — player only);
    GTM MX-50 (24), GTM Fury (16)
  - Alpha 2–4 loadout: GTW ML-16 Laser x2; GTM MX-50 (24), GTM Fury (16)
  - Player is Alpha 1 with the field Avenger. Wingmen do NOT have it — this is deliberate.
    The player will feel the difference immediately.
  - Initial orders: Escort Theta wing to Nav Alpha

- PVD Vigil (PVD Typhon-class): Entering from Capella node at T+00:20
  - Status: Will be destroyed within 2–3 minutes of arrival regardless of player action.
    The Vigil has no fighter cover. She is outnumbered and caught in an ambush she anticipated
    but couldn't avoid. Brahma wing focuses on her first, then the transports.
  - The Vigil's death is SCRIPTED. Players cannot save her. She will engage Brahma and Rama
    wings as long as she can, drawing fire away from Theta.
  - Sub-Imperator Hethor persona: brief, stoic, unflinching. One message before she goes silent.

- Theta 1, 2, 3 (PVT Isis x3): Entering from Capella node with the Vigil
  - Speed: Moderate for a transport (Isis is faster than GTT Elysium). They can maneuver.
  - Isis transports have 3 turrets each — they're not helpless, but not fighters.
  - Theta 1 leads. Navigation target: Nav Alpha, then they can jump independently from there.
  - Theta 3 is the slowest (minor engine damage logged in fiction viewer). Most at-risk.
  - If all 3 reach Nav Alpha they jump to Antares on their own. Mission complete once Nav Alpha
    reached (player does not need to escort them through; just get them clear of the engagement).

- GTS Casca (GTS Centaur-class): Available for rearm on request from T+06:00 onward
  - Casca inbound from the Amadeus — not present at mission start. Player must hold on.

Hostile:
- Brahma wing: SF Scorpion x4
  - Present at mission start, converging on Capella node approach (3 km flank approach)
  - Initial orders: Attack PVD Vigil (primary), Attack Theta wing (secondary)
  - This wave splits attention between destroying the Vigil (fast) and pursuing Theta (slower).
    Player can't stop the Vigil's death but can stop Brahma from following through to Theta.

- Aquarius wing (HoL): PVF Horus x4
  - Present at mission start on the far side of the node, moving to intercept Theta
  - Initial orders: Attack Theta wing
  - The HoL fighters are here specifically to prevent any Vasudan refugee from escaping —
    their approach vector cuts across the line from Capella node to Antares corridor.
  - HoL do NOT have Shivan shields. They're slightly tougher than stock Vasudans but killable
    with sustained ML-16 fire. The Avenger eats through them.
  - First HoL encounter in campaign. Briefing warned about them. Now the player acts on it.

- Pisces wing (HoL): PVF Seth x3
  - Arrives at Nav Alpha approach (35,000m from current position, 8 km from Nav Alpha) at T+04:00
  - Initial orders: Guard Nav Alpha approach, Attack any Theta ships approaching
  - The Seths are slower but this is a blocking position. They bought it early and are dug in.
  - Player must clear them from Nav Alpha before Theta can reach it safely — or lead Theta through
    while simultaneously engaging. Design tension: go ahead and clear vs. stay with transports.

- Rama wing: SF Basilisk x3
  - Arrives from Capella direction at T+05:00 or when PVD Vigil is destroyed (whichever is first)
  - Initial orders: Attack Theta wing
  - The Basilisk wave is the last push. After the Vigil is gone, they come for the transports.

PLAYER CRAFT AND LOADOUT
- Ship: GTF Valkyrie (recommended) or GTF Apollo
- Primary: GTW-15 Avenger (bank 1, PLAYER ONLY — force this), GTW ML-16 (bank 2)
- Secondary: GTM MX-50 (24), GTM Fury (16)
- No forced loadout — player chooses. But briefing notes that Alpha 1 has the Avenger and
  should use it to generate performance data.
- GTS Casca available from T+06:00. Not present at start.

OBJECTIVES AND DIRECTIVES

Primary:
  - At least 2 of 3 Theta transports reach Nav Alpha alive.

Secondary:
  - Destroy Pisces wing (HoL blocking force) before Theta wing reaches Nav Alpha.
    (Rewards proactive play — go ahead and clear the path rather than just escort.)

Bonus:
  - All 3 Theta transports survive to Nav Alpha. (Full evacuation — rare but achievable.)

Failure conditions:
  - Fewer than 2 Theta transports reach Nav Alpha.
  - (Alpha 1 destroyed = standard pilot death.)

EVENT LOGIC SKETCH
- "PVD Vigil destroyed" event: Scripted — Vigil has deliberately lowered hull value at start.
  Brahma wing's damage output will destroy it within 2 minutes. Message 6 fires on this event.
  After this event, Brahma's target priority shifts from Vigil to Theta wing.
- "Aquarius wing engage Theta" event: fires when Aquarius ships are within 2,000m of any
  Theta ship. Message 2 fires shortly after.
- "Pisces wing detected" event: fires when any Alpha ship is within 15,000m of Nav Alpha,
  or at T+04:00, whichever comes first. One-time trigger.
- "Rama wing arrival" event: fires at T+05:00 OR when PVD Vigil is destroyed, first wins.
- "GTS Casca available" event fires at T+06:00.
- "Mission success" condition: 2 or more Theta ships have "jumped from Nav Alpha" flag set.
- "Mission failure" condition: Theta ships with "survived" status drops below 2.
- Theta ships at Nav Alpha: When any Theta ship reaches Nav Alpha, it immediately begins a
  jump-out sequence (10-second count). Player must clear threats before Theta arrives, or
  escort through them. Theta ships will not hold at Nav Alpha waiting for clearance.

NEWLY INTRODUCED SHIPS AND WEAPONS
- GTW-15 Avenger: First in-combat use. Player has it in bank 1 as the only Avenger in Alpha wing.
  This is the key mechanical introduction of the act — the first moment Shivans start dying at
  a reasonable rate. The contrast with ML-16 should be notable but not game-breaking. Shivans
  still require focus and situational awareness to kill.
- PVD Typhon (PVD Vigil): First Vasudan destroyer in campaign. Appears only to be destroyed.
  Her death is designed to be impactful, not gratuitous — the fiction viewer makes it matter.
- PVT Isis: First Vasudan transport class. Theta wing. The player has been protecting GTT
  Elysium ships; these are different. Mechanically similar but visually/contextually distinct.
- PVF Horus (HoL Aquarius wing), PVF Seth (HoL Pisces wing): First Hammer of Light contact
  in campaign. Distinguished from PVN by HoL markings — player must register the difference.

DEBRIEFINGS

Full success (all 3 Theta transports survived, Pisces wing destroyed):
Theta wing made it through. Two of three of those transports are carrying civilian Vasudans from
what used to be a PVN forward outpost. They'll reach Antares.
The Vigil is gone. Sub-Imperator Hethor ordered her transports to break formation before she
made her stand at the Capella node. She didn't have to do that. She knew the odds. She did it
anyway.
There's no alliance in the books yet. No ceasefire. But today, Alpha wing and the crew of Theta
wing were working toward the same thing. I'm noting that in my report to Command.
The Avenger performance data has been forwarded to R&D. We'll have more units within three days.

Partial success (2 Theta transports survived):
Two transports made it to Antares. We lost one.
The Vigil held as long as she could. Her commander understood what she was giving and gave it.
I don't have a better summary of what happened today than that.
The Avenger data is useful. More weapons are coming. I want you thinking about what that means
for how we fly — faster engagement, cleaner kills, less time at risk. Study the recordings.

Failure (fewer than 2 Theta transports survived):
The Theta wing transports didn't make it. Sub-Imperator Hethor gave everything her ship had to
buy them time, and it wasn't enough.
We can account for the tactical situation — HoL blocking force at Nav Alpha, Rama wing timing —
but accounting for it doesn't change what happened. Those were civilian ships.
R&D has the Avenger data. We'll continue the withdrawal planning. Right now there's nothing
else to say.

QA NOTES
- PVD Vigil's death must be guaranteed within 2 minutes. Vigil hull should be set low at spawn
  (roughly 35–40% hull) and Brahma wing's attack priority on her must be hard. If the Vigil
  somehow survives longer than 3 minutes, it breaks the mission's emotional arc.
- Player must NOT be able to save the Vigil. This is a core design rule. If players report
  finding a way to protect the Vigil, the hull setting or Brahma priority must be adjusted.
- Aquarius wing (HoL) must reach Theta before the player can fully deal with Brahma. The
  timeline overlap is intentional — it creates prioritization pressure.
- Theta 3 needs a slightly lower speed than Theta 1 and 2 to create a naturally dramatic "last
  to arrive" moment. The message about Theta 3 being slowest (Alpha 2, Rama wing trigger) adds
  to this.
- The Theta 1 transmission at Nav Alpha (Sub-Imperator's compliments) is the emotional climax
  of the act. It must play cleanly. Ensure no other message can overlap it within 10 seconds.
- Pisces wing at Nav Alpha is a blocking element. If the player engages them proactively (going
  ahead of Theta to clear the path), the secondary objective is achievable. If the player stays
  with Theta at all times, the Seths will catch up with the transports as they arrive at Nav Alpha
  — harder but still doable. Both are valid play styles.
- GTS Casca availability at T+06:00: calibrate so Casca arrives just as the Rama wave is most
  dangerous. Being able to rearm at exactly the right moment is a gameplay reward.
