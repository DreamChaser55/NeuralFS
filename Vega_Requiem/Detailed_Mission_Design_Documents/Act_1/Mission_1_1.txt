================================================================================
COMMAND BRIEFING 1.1
"Steady Hands"
================================================================================

Delivered aboard: GTD Amadeus
Location: Vega System
Date: January 15, 2335

--- STAGE 1: GARRISON STATUS ---
Attention, pilots. Welcome to the 0800 brief. I'm Rear Admiral Chen Wei, commanding the Amadeus
and the 3rd Fleet detachment in the Vega system.

We have now held this system for eleven months. The Terran-Vasudan War is grinding on in its fourteenth year. Our job here is unchanged: patrol the four jump node approaches, maintain system
integrity, and protect GTA assets. The GTI Vega Prime installation remains operational and is
currently handling the last civilian evacuation shuttles from the Vega colony platforms — routine
administrative work that I want completed without incident.

The war is still raging. Until Terran Command says otherwise, standard rules of engagement remain in effect.
Vasudan contacts inside Vega are to be treated as hostile. No exceptions.

--- STAGE 2: PATROL ASSIGNMENT ---
We lost contact with the patrol transport GTT Meridian at 0340 this morning. She was running a
standard pass along the Capella approach corridor, approximately 15,000 meters from the
Capella jump node. Her last beacon ping was normal. Then nothing.

This is most likely a systems failure. Meridian's drive was flagged for maintenance two weeks ago.
However, standing orders require that all unexplained beacon silences be investigated within six
hours.

Alpha wing — that's you, Pilot — you're going out to investigate. Four ships, standard loadout.
Check on Meridian, confirm her status, and return. If she's adrift, note the position and we'll
send a salvage tug. If there's Vasudan activity in that corridor, report it and do not escalate
unless fired upon first.

--- STAGE 3: NOTES FOR NEW PILOTS ---
If this is your first duty rotation on the Amadeus, welcome. This is not a glamorous posting.
Vega is a four-node intersection that hasn't seen a major fleet engagement in seven months.
Most of your work here will be exactly what Alpha wing is doing today: investigating silences,
protecting convoys, holding the line.

Do it well. The Amadeus has a reputation, and I intend to keep it.

Dismissed. Report to your wing station at 0820.

MISSION DESIGN DOCUMENT
Mission 1-1: "Strange Fires"
================================================================================

PURPOSE AND PLAYER EXPERIENCE
- Mission fantasy: A routine investigation that goes catastrophically wrong. The player has the
  full confidence of an experienced combat pilot — and the Shivans shred it in seconds.
- Core tension: The weapons don't work. Everything the player knows about space combat is
  insufficient. Survival is the only achievable goal.
- Plot advance: Establishes the Shivans as an existential threat. Creates the emotional baseline
  of helplessness and confusion that all subsequent escalation is measured against.
- This mission should feel like the scene in a horror film where the monster is first revealed —
  and it's worse than anything the audience imagined.

FICTION VIEWER NARRATIVE
- None for this mission. The command briefing delivers all necessary setup.
- The horror should arrive unannounced in-mission, with no fiction viewer preparing the player.

ENVIRONMENT AND SETUP
- Datestamp: January 15, 2335
- System: Vega — deep space, Capella approach corridor
- Location: 15,000 m from the Vega-Capella jump node, far from any installation
- Background: Deep blue-black starfield; Vega's star a bright point astern; the faint shimmer
  of the Capella node visible at long range as a navigational reference point
- No installations, no buoys, no mines in this area
- Hazard notes: None (open space). The emptiness is deliberate — no cover, nowhere to hide.
- GTT Meridian: Already destroyed before mission begins. Her wreckage (scored hull plating,
  scattered cargo) occupies a 500m debris field at Nav Alpha. Two GTEP Hermes escape pods
  drift near the wreckage, broadcasting distress beacons.

PARTICIPANTS

Allied:
- Alpha wing: GTF Apollo x4 (Alpha 1 = player, Alpha 2-4 = AI)
  - Loadout: GTW ML-16 Laser x2; GTM MX-50 (16), GTM Fury (16)
  - Initial orders: Fly to Nav Alpha; investigate GTT Meridian

- Escape Pod 1 (GTEP Hermes): Drifting at Nav Alpha, 200m from Meridian wreckage
- Escape Pod 2 (GTEP Hermes): Drifting at Nav Alpha, 350m from Meridian wreckage
  - Both pods navigate autonomously toward the jump-out waypoint once mission begins
  - Speed: Very slow. Without player protection they are vulnerable.

- No support ship available. This is a patrol sortie.

Hostile:
- Brahma wing: SF Scorpion x4
  - Arrives at Nav Alpha from the direction of the Capella node at 8 km distance
  - Trigger: Alpha wing within 3,000m of Meridian wreckage
  - Initial orders: Attack Alpha wing, Attack escape pods (secondary target)
  - Scorpion shields require prolonged ML-16 fire — approximately 3–4 seconds of direct hits per
    shield quad. This is noticeably worse than any Vasudan fighter the player has fought.

- Vishnu wing: SF Basilisk x3
  - Arrives from Capella direction at 10 km, 90 seconds after Brahma wing arrives OR when Brahma
    wing falls below 2 surviving ships (whichever comes first)
  - Initial orders: Attack Alpha wing, Attack escape pods
  - Basilisks are heavier and hit harder than Scorpions. Their arrival is the trigger for retreat.

PLAYER CRAFT AND LOADOUT
- Ship: GTF Apollo
- Primary weapons: GTW ML-16 Laser (bank 1 + bank 2)
- Secondary weapons: GTM MX-50 (16), GTM Fury (16)
- No support ship
- No reinforcements available
- No Avenger cannon this mission — its absence is the design point.
- Note: MX-50 is heat-seeking but weak. Fury is dumbfire close-range. Neither is reliable
  against Shivan shields at the distances these fights will be fought.

OBJECTIVES AND DIRECTIVES
Primary:
  - SURVIVE: Alpha 1 reaches the jump-out waypoint alive.
  - Required for mission completion.

Secondary:
  - Both Escape Pod 1 and Escape Pod 2 reach the jump-out waypoint.
  - Pods navigate slowly toward the waypoint on their own. Player must protect them from
    Brahma/Vishnu fire while also surviving.
  - Partial credit: One pod saved = partial success noted in debrief.

Failure conditions:
  - Alpha 1 destroyed.

BRIEFINGS (TEXT-READY)

MISSION BRIEFING

Stage 1:
GTD Amadeus Operations, this is your mission package. GTT Meridian's beacon last pinged at these
coordinates in the Capella corridor. You'll jump in approximately six kilometers from her last
known position.
We don't know what happened to her. This is a recon and recovery assignment. If she's adrift, pull
her crew manifest and assess damage. If there's Vasudan activity in the area, follow standard
rules of engagement.
Expected duration: thirty minutes. Come home clean.

Stage 2:
Alpha wing, you're cleared to the Capella corridor. Nav Alpha marks the Meridian's last position.
You'll jump in approximately six kilometers from her last known position. Proceed there, assess
the situation, and return when complete. Don't get ambitious out there.

[ Briefing icon notes: Nav Alpha marked at Meridian's position. Jump-out waypoint marked at
  mission origin. No other waypoints. Simple there-and-back layout. ]

IN-MISSION COMMUNICATIONS

Message 1 — Alpha 2, persona: veteran wingman (calm, steady), trigger: Alpha wing within 1,000m
of Meridian wreckage
"Alpha Lead, I'm reading — that's wreckage. Hull plating, looks like Meridian. She's gone."

Message 2 — Alpha 1 (player persona, triggered by engine): Automatic target designation message
[System] — 2 escape pods detected. Distress transponders active. Pods are alive.

Message 3 — Alpha 3, persona: younger pilot (nervous edge), trigger: Brahma wing arrival
"Contact! Multiple bogeys from Capella bearing. They're — they're not Vasudan."

Message 4 — Alpha 2, trigger: 10 seconds after Brahma wing arrival
"Firing Avenger— no, wait, I've got ML-16s loaded. These shields are eating it. Push through."

Message 5 — Alpha 2, trigger: 30 seconds after Brahma engagement, if any enemy ship survives
"These things don't die fast enough. Stay tight, Lead. Don't let them get behind you."

Message 6 — Command (Amadeus CAG, persona: professional, controlled alarm), trigger: Vishnu
wing arrival (or 90 seconds after Brahma)
"Alpha Lead, Command. We're reading a second wave at your position. Disengage. Break off and
return immediately. Do NOT pursue. Repeat — do not pursue."

Message 7 — Alpha 4, persona: frightened but professional, trigger: Vishnu wing arrival if
Alpha 3 or 4 is already destroyed
"[Name of lost pilot], report! — Command, we're down to [number] ships. Request—"
Command: "Alpha Lead, get your people home NOW."

Message 8 — Alpha 2, trigger: if both escape pods are alive when Alpha wing turns for jump-out
"Pods are still moving. They might make it if we buy them thirty seconds."

Message 9 — Command, trigger: player within 2,000m of jump-out waypoint
"Alpha Lead, jump when ready. We'll send salvage for the pods if they don't make it. Get home."

Message 10 — Alpha 2, trigger: mission end (player jumping out)
"Amadeus, Alpha Lead is inbound. We need to talk about what we just saw."

EVENT FLOW AND TIMING
- T+00:00: Mission starts. Alpha wing at jump-in point, 6 km from Nav Alpha. No enemies visible.
- T+00:00 to T+01:15: Alpha wing flies to Meridian wreckage (Nav Alpha). Quiet. The wreckage
  is visually dramatic — scored metal, scattered cargo. Escape pods drifting.
- T+01:15 (approx): Alpha wing within 3,000m of wreckage. Triggers Brahma wing arrival from
  Capella node direction (8 km out). Message 3 fires.
- T+01:15 to T+04:15: Combat with Brahma wing (4x Scorpion). Player fires ML-16. It's slow.
  Each Scorpion takes 3–4 seconds of sustained fire to drop a shield quad. AI wingmen struggle.
  Alpha 3 and/or 4 may be destroyed in this window depending on difficulty.
- T+04:15 (or when Brahma drops to 2 ships): Vishnu wing arrives (3x Basilisk, 10 km).
  Message 6 fires: Command orders retreat.
- T+04:15 onward: Escape pods are navigating toward jump-out waypoint. Player can either
  cover them or immediately run. Fighting Vishnu + reduced Brahma simultaneously is very
  dangerous with ML-16s.
- T+06:00 to T+09:00 (approx): Surviving Alpha ships reach jump-out waypoint and exit.
  If escape pods reach the waypoint by this time, secondary objective achieved.

EVENT LOGIC SKETCH
- "Brahma wing arrives" event: fires when Alpha 1 is within 3,000m of GTT Meridian wreckage.
  One-time trigger.
- "Vishnu wing arrives" event: fires when mission time since Brahma arrival exceeds 90 seconds,
  OR when Brahma wing's living ship count drops to 2 or fewer. First condition met triggers.
- "Escape pod 1 safe" event: fires when Escape Pod 1 is within 500m of jump-out waypoint.
- "Escape pod 2 safe" event: fires when Escape Pod 2 is within 500m of jump-out waypoint.
- "Secondary objective complete" event: fires when both pod-safe events have fired.
- "Mission success" condition: Alpha 1 reaches jump-out waypoint (arrives within 500m and
  initiates jump). No time limit for the mission overall.
- All enemy wings on "attack" orders targeting Alpha wing, with escape pods as secondary
  target priority. Pods have no weapons and will not maneuver to evade — they drift slowly.

NEWLY INTRODUCED SHIPS AND WEAPONS
- SF Scorpion: First player encounter in this campaign.
- SF Basilisk: First player encounter in this campaign (Vishnu wave).
- GTT Meridian: Appears as wreckage only. Never seen alive.
- GTEP Hermes: First appearance in campaign. Establishes that escape pods are a recurring
  element that players will care about in later missions.
- GTW ML-16 Laser: Player is already familiar. This mission teaches via trauma that it is
  inadequate against Shivan shields.
- Note: NO new tech is introduced in this mission. Absence is the lesson.

DEBRIEFINGS

Success (Alpha 1 survived, both pods saved):
You made it back. The sensor data from your engagement has been transmitted to Terran Command.
We have never encountered ships matching these profiles in any GTA database. They are not
Vasudan, and they are not anything we've seen before.
Commander's evaluation: your ML-16 rounds were largely ineffective against their shields. You
know this. We know this. R&D is being alerted.
Meridian's survivors are being treated aboard the Amadeus. Their account matches your sensor
data: these ships came from the direction of the Capella node. We do not know what is through
that node.
Well done for getting the pods home. We will have more answers by morning. I wish I could
promise better news when we do.

Partial success (Alpha 1 survived, one pod saved):
Alpha Lead, welcome back. You got one of the Meridian's pods out. The other is presumed lost.
I want you to understand that those were not typical combat conditions. Our weapons were not
designed for whatever we just encountered, and this was not a failure of tactics. You were
handed the wrong tools for the job and you still brought people home.
We're sending all your sensor recordings to Command immediately. Whatever those ships are,
we need answers fast.

Failure (Alpha 1 survived but no pods saved):
Alpha Lead, glad you're back. The pods are lost. The crew of the Meridian will be listed as
killed in action. That is a hard outcome, but given what you faced out there, I'm not
prepared to call this your fault. Those shields absorbed everything you had.
The data you brought back is the only thing we came away with. It had better count for
something.

QA NOTES
- Ensure Brahma wing spawns from a direction that makes sense (toward Capella node) and not
  behind the player's entry point.
- Escape pod movement speed must be calibrated to allow player to cover them while also
  fighting Brahma wing — if pods are too slow they are impossible to save while also surviving
  the Vishnu wave.
- Alpha AI wingmen need "protect Alpha 1" as primary order, not "attack enemy" — this keeps them
  near the player rather than charging Scorpions and dying immediately, which would be
  frustrating rather than horrifying.
- On Insane difficulty: Scorpions have near-player accuracy. Player cannot tank hits. Flight-or-
  fight decisions become critical. With only ML-16s, every second of engagement matters.
  The escape pod secondary should be achievable but requires commitment — player cannot both
  immediately flee AND save both pods. This is a design tension, not an oversight.
- Do not spawn Vishnu wing inside 5 km — the player needs a moment to see them on approach.
