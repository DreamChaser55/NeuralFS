================================================================================
MISSION DESIGN DOCUMENT
Mission 1-2: "Breakwater"
================================================================================

PURPOSE AND PLAYER EXPERIENCE
- Mission fantasy: The player is the last wall between civilians and something that cannot be
  reasoned with. Every second of delay bought against the Shivan advance is a life saved.
- Core tension: Split priority. Epsilon transports must reach the jump node. GTC Stentor must survive.
  The SC Malgor's arrival mid-mission reframes the threat from fighter swarm to capital assault.
- Plot advance: GTI Vega Prime falls, confirming no static defense can hold. The evacuation
  succeeds well enough to matter — this is a salvage of life from a strategic defeat.
- Emotional register: Controlled desperation. The station is going to die; the question is
  whether anyone has to die with it. Introduce GTF Valkyrie as the tool that makes a difference.
- This mission should feel like a desperate rearguard action where every kill buys time, and the
  player comes away bruised but not broken. A partial victory that stings less than a defeat.

FICTION VIEWER NARRATIVE
- Displayed before this mission.
- File: Mission_1_2_story.txt
- Summary: A station technician stranded on GTI Vega Prime's lower decks during the evacuation.
  Follows her route to the last remaining escape pod. Tone: quiet horror, human detail, survival.
- Purpose: Puts a face on the "civilians" the player is protecting. When the transports escape
  in-mission, the player knows someone real might be on them.

ENVIRONMENT AND SETUP
- Datestamp: January 17, 2335
- System: Vega — GTI Vega Prime installation vicinity
- Location: 5,000m from GTI Vega Prime (GTI Arcadia-class); Vega star system inner zone
- Background: Warm amber-orange starfield; Vega's main sequence star large and dominant in the
  upper quadrant; Vega Prime's superstructure visible and increasingly battered across mission
- Objects in scene at mission start:
  - GTI Vega Prime (GTI Arcadia-class): Present and operational at mission start; heavily damaged
    by midpoint; destroyed before mission ends. Center of the scene. Multiple docking arms active.
  - GTC Stentor (GTC Fenris-class): Positioned 3,000m from Vega Prime on the approach side,
    firing at incoming Shivan fighters and serving as picket line.
  - Nav Alpha: 2,000m from Vega Prime — the rally point for Epsilon transports before their jump.
  - Nav Bravo: The Vega-Antares subspace jump node approach, 9,000m from station.
  - Epsilon wing (GTT Elysium x4): Docked at Vega Prime at mission start. Undock staggered across
    first 3 minutes, then form up at Nav Alpha before proceeding to Nav Bravo.
  - Three TAC-1 cargo containers at docking arm 3 (civilian equipment — irrelevant to objectives
    but scannable as bonus; confirms civilian nature of station).
  - Four Terran NavBuoys marking the approach corridor to the Antares node.
  - Several GTSG Watchdog sentry guns around the station (3–4); they will be destroyed quickly by
    the Shivans, but their presence establishes that someone tried to defend this place properly.

PARTICIPANTS

Allied:
- Alpha wing: GTF Valkyrie x4 (Alpha 1 = player, Alpha 2–4 = AI)
  - Loadout: GTW ML-16 Laser x2; GTM MX-50 (24), GTM Fury (16)
  - Note: Valkyrie is available for the first time this mission. Its speed advantage is
    immediately relevant for intercept runs against the bomber Arjuna wave.
  - Initial orders: Protect Epsilon wing; Protect GTC Stentor

- GTC Stentor (GTC Fenris-class): Present at mission start, engaging Durga wing
  - Commanding officer: Commander Yael Okafor (persona: naval professional, pragmatic)
  - Hull at start: 100%. Must finish above 50% for secondary objective.
  - Stentor's guns engage Durga wing throughout early mission. When SC Malgor arrives, Stentor
    turns to engage it at close range — suicidal, but necessary to give the transports time.
  - Orders: Protect GTI Vega Prime, then Protect Epsilon wing (shifts when station destroyed)

- Epsilon 1–4 (GTT Elysium-class transports): Undock staggered from Vega Prime
- Epsilon 1 departs at T+01:30; Epsilon 2 at T+02:00; Epsilon 3 at T+02:45; Epsilon 4 at T+03:30
- All four navigate to Nav Alpha, then proceed to Nav Bravo (Antares node)
- Speed: Slow. Nav Alpha is 30 seconds at transport speed. Nav Bravo is approximately 4 minutes.
- Epsilon 3 and Epsilon 4 are the most vulnerable — they depart later when Shivan pressure is highest.

- GTS Casca (GTS Centaur-class support ship): Arrives from Amadeus direction at T+04:00
  - Player may call Casca for rearming once per mission.
  - Casca departs after rearm cycle or when all Epsilon transports are at Nav Bravo, whichever
    comes first. Casca is fragile — player should not let Shivans focus on it.

- GTI Vega Prime (GTI Arcadia-class): Installation, not pilotable
  - Hull: 100% at start. Reduced to ~30% by T+06:00 by Arjuna bomber wave.
  - Destroyed (explodes with spectacular effect) at approximately T+08:00–T+10:00 depending on
    player protection. Player cannot prevent this. The station's destruction is scripted — what
    varies is the exact timing, which affects whether Epsilon 3 and 4 are still in danger.

Hostile:
- Durga wing: SF Scorpion x4
  - Present at mission start, already attacking GTI Vega Prime and GTC Stentor
  - Initial orders: Attack GTI Vega Prime; Attack GTC Stentor
  - These are the first contacts. Player must deal with them quickly before the bomber wave.

- Arjuna wing: SF Basilisk x3 (first wave)
  - Arrives at T+02:30 from Capella node direction (12 km)
  - Initial orders: Attack GTI Vega Prime (concentrate fire on docking arms)
  - These are the station killers. Player can intercept them but cannot prevent the station's fall.

- Arjuna wing second wave (SF Basilisk x3):
  - Arrives when Arjuna first wave falls to 1 ship OR at T+05:30, whichever comes first
  - Initial orders: Attack Epsilon wing (transitions from station to transports once station is dying)
  - This wave is the primary threat to mission success — it targets the transports directly.

- Krishna wing: SF Scorpion x3
  - Arrives at T+04:30 from Capella direction (10 km)
  - Initial orders: Attack Alpha wing
  - Pure fighter harassment — intended to pull Alpha away from bomber intercept duty.

- SC Malgor (SC Cain-class cruiser): Arrives at T+06:30 from Capella node (12 km)
  - Initial orders: Attack GTI Vega Prime
  - The arrival of a capital ship reframes the mission — the station was doomed before, now
    it's final. The Malgor turns toward the Antares node approach corridor at T+08:00 to
    harass Epsilon wing from range. It cannot prevent the transports from jumping but creates
    pressure and provides a target for a secondary objective (destroy it) in Mission 2-3.
  - Malgor has standard Cain-class turrets: multiple light plasma batteries, cluster bomb
    defensive mechanism. The cluster bomb is only triggered if a player ship closes within 400m.
  - Note: The Malgor is NOT a mission-end target here. Destroying it is not required and is
    extremely difficult without bomber support. Players who try to engage it directly with fighters
    will fail secondary objectives. The design lesson: know what you can and can't fight.

PLAYER CRAFT AND LOADOUT
- Ship: GTF Valkyrie (first appearance; highly recommended for its intercept speed)
- Primary: GTW ML-16 Laser x2
- Secondary: GTM MX-50 (24), GTM Fury (16)
- Support: GTS Casca (one rearm available, arrives T+04:00)
- No reinforcements available by callout. This is what you have.
- Valkyrie note for briefing: "She's fast. Use that."

OBJECTIVES AND DIRECTIVES

Primary:
  - At least 3 of 4 Epsilon transports reach Nav Bravo (Antares jump node) and jump out.

Secondary:
  - GTC Stentor hull remains above 50% when all Epsilon transports have departed.

Bonus:
  - All 4 Epsilon transports survive to jump out. (Full evacuation success.)
  - Scan bonus cargo containers at Vega Prime docking arm 3 (reveals civilian equipment,
    acknowledged in debrief with commendation for thoroughness).

Failure conditions:
  - Fewer than 3 Epsilon transports reach Nav Bravo.
  - (Alpha 1 destroyed = standard pilot death failure.)

BRIEFINGS (TEXT-READY)

MISSION BRIEFING

Stage 1:
Alpha wing, listen up. The situation has changed overnight. What Alpha Lead brought back from
the Capella corridor wasn't Vasudan, wasn't anything Command could identify — and this morning
at 0530, those same contact profiles showed up in force at the GTI Vega Prime perimeter.
We have four Epsilon transports at Vega Prime right now — civilian contractors, administrative
personnel, maintenance crews. They need to get out. The transports are undocking now.
GTC Stentor is already in the fight. Your job is to keep the fighters off those transports long
enough for all four of them to clear the system at the Antares node.

Stage 2:
You'll be flying Valkyries today. They just came in from the Amadeus complement — fast
interceptors, good for exactly this kind of work. Use the speed. Get between the bombers and
the transports. The station is already taking damage and we don't expect to hold it. That's not
your mission. The people on those transports are your mission.
Nav Alpha is the rendezvous point between the station and the node corridor. The transports will
stage there before making their run to Nav Bravo. Get them through.

Stage 3:
One more thing. We're sending the Casca out behind you for rearming support. She's fragile —
don't let her become a target. Call her in when you need her, not before.
The Stentor will hold as long as she can. Commander Okafor knows her job.
Good luck. GTA Amadeus CAG out.

EVENT FLOW AND TIMING
- T+00:00: Mission begins. GTI Vega Prime under attack. Durga wing (Scorpion x4) engaging
  station and Stentor. Alpha wing at jump-in point 6 km from station.
- T+01:30: Epsilon 1 undocks from Vega Prime. Begins navigation to Nav Alpha.
- T+02:00: Epsilon 2 undocks. Begins navigation to Nav Alpha.
- T+02:30: Arjuna wave 1 (Basilisk x3) arrives from Capella, 12 km. Targets Vega Prime.
- T+02:45: Epsilon 3 undocks.
- T+03:00: Epsilon 1 and 2 reach Nav Alpha and begin proceeding to Nav Bravo (7 km at
  transport speed — approximately 3.5 minutes of travel).
- T+03:30: Epsilon 4 undocks.
- T+04:00: GTS Casca arrives in scene (available for rearm requests from this point).
- T+04:30: Krishna wing (Scorpion x3) arrives from Capella, 10 km. Targets Alpha wing.
- T+05:30 (or when Arjuna wave 1 drops to 1 ship): Arjuna wave 2 (Basilisk x3) arrives.
  NOW targets Epsilon wing instead of station. Critical intercept window.
- T+06:30: SC Malgor (Cain-class) arrives from Capella, 12 km. Targets station.
  Vega Prime is at ~30% hull or lower by now.
- T+07:00: SC Malgor changes target priority to Antares corridor / Epsilon transports
  once it closes to within 6 km of the station. Stentor engages Malgor at close range.
- T+07:00–T+09:00: GTI Vega Prime is destroyed (exact timing varies slightly).
- T+06:30–T+09:00: Surviving Epsilon transports reach Nav Bravo and jump to Antares.
  Mission ends when all surviving Epsilon ships have jumped.

EVENT LOGIC SKETCH
- "Epsilon 1 undock" event fires at T+01:30. Subsequent transports fire at fixed intervals.
- "Arjuna wave 2 arrival" event fires when Arjuna wave 1 ships-alive count is 1 OR when
  T+05:30 is reached. First trigger wins.
- "Krishna wing arrival" fires at T+04:30 unconditionally.
- "SC Malgor arrival" fires at T+06:30 unconditionally.
- "SC Malgor changes target" fires at T+08:00 unconditionally. AI goal reassignment.
- "Vega Prime destruction" is scripted — Vega Prime has a hull integrity track. With Durga,
  Arjuna wave 1, and SC Malgor all targeting it, it will be destroyed between T+08:00 and
  T+10:00 regardless of player action. Player cannot protect the station.
- "Mission complete" condition fires when all Epsilon ships with status "survived" have their
  "has jumped out" flag set, and count of survived Epsilon ships >= 3.
- "Mission failure" fires if Epsilon survived count drops below 3 (i.e., 2 or fewer survive).
  This requires 3 or more of the 4 transports to be destroyed, which is very unlikely unless
  the player actively ignores Arjuna wave 2.

NEWLY INTRODUCED SHIPS AND WEAPONS
- GTF Valkyrie: First available in campaign. Introduced in briefing stage 2.
  Default recommendation. Speed is the mechanical hook for this mission.
- SC Malgor (SC Cain-class): First capital ship threat of the campaign.
  NOT engageable by player in this mission — establishes that capital ships require bombers.
- GTC Stentor: Player's allied cruiser, first real appearance (was mentioned in CB 1.1).
  Commander Okafor established as a named persona for subsequent missions.
- GTI Vega Prime (GTI Arcadia-class): The installation the player is "defending" — its
  destruction mid-mission is a deliberately designed moment of loss.

DEBRIEFINGS

Full success (all 4 Epsilon transports survived, Stentor above 50%):
All four Epsilon transports are safely through the Antares node. The station is gone — but the
people are not. That matters.
Commander Okafor sends her thanks. The Stentor took some damage, but she held together, and
her crew held together with her. She says you covered her exactly when she needed it.
Vega Prime is lost. We knew it was coming, but knowing doesn't make it easier. I want you to
understand that everything you defended out there had a name and a family. You did right by
them today.
The Malgor is still in the system. We're keeping track of her.

Partial success (3 of 4 transports survived, or Stentor below 50%):
Three transports made it. That's people alive who would otherwise be dead, and I'm not going
to diminish that. But we lost [one/the Stentor took heavy damage], and I want you to think
about what you could have done differently.
Arjuna wave — that second bomber group — came in fast. The Valkyrie was the right choice, but
the intercept timing was tight. Study the sensor recording. It'll matter next time.
Vega Prime is gone. The Malgor is still in the system. The situation is deteriorating.

Failure (fewer than 3 transports survived):
This is hard to report. Of the four Epsilon transports, only [count] made it. The others were
destroyed before they could reach the jump node. Those were civilian evacuation ships.
I have to be direct with you: the Arjuna bombers were the mission-critical threat, and they
were not intercepted quickly enough. I understand what you were facing — those shields, that
SC Malgor arriving — but our job was the transports.
We will not have another chance in Vega. What we do from here is damage limitation. Regroup.

QA NOTES
- The mission is designed so the player CANNOT prevent Vega Prime's destruction. This must be
  communicated clearly — either in the briefing or via early in-mission dialogue. Players who
  attempt to protect the station at the expense of the transports will fail the primary objective.
  The message from Vega Prime Control should make clear the station is accepting its fate.
- Arjuna wave 2 transition from "attack station" to "attack Epsilon transports" is the critical
  design beat. The timing of its arrival relative to when Epsilon 1 and 2 leave Nav Alpha must be
  calibrated so that the player faces a genuine decision — the fighters threatening their wing
  (Krishna) versus the bombers threatening the transports (Arjuna wave 2).
- SC Malgor must not be targetable as a mission objective. Its turrets should not be lethal to
  fighters at range (Cain-class has plasma batteries, not precision anti-fighter weapons at
  distance). Players who close to within 400m trigger the cluster bomb — this should be
  educational rather than immediately fatal.
- GTS Casca is vulnerable and should be positioned near Vega Prime (not on the Capella side).
  If Shivans acquire it as a target, it will die quickly. The player can draw them away.
- Epsilon 3 and 4 are the emotionally weighted transports — they depart last, are most exposed,
  and Epsilon 3 has a panicked pilot voice line. Player attachment is intentional.
