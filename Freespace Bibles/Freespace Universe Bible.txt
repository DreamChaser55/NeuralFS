Freespace Universe

FreeSpace is a space combat simulation series by Volition, Inc. and Interplay Entertainment. Set in the 24th century, humans (Terrans) have developed faster-than-light space travel via subspace, enabling interstellar expansion. Terrans soon encounter the Vasudans, another sentient species, but cultural differences lead to a 14-year war between the Galactic Terran Alliance (GTA) and the Parliamentary Vasudan Empire (PVE).

FreeSpace 1:
FreeSpace — The Great War, also known as Conflict: Freespace or Freespace 1 (FS1), was released in 1998. Player is a pilot for the Galactic Terran Alliance (GTA), which has been at war with the Parliamentary Vasudan Navy (PVN) for 14 years. A third race, the technologically superior and enigmatic Shivans, suddenly appears, attacking both sides. This forces the GTA and PVN to form a truce to combat the Shivans. FS1 was well-received for its solid gameplay, controls, and compelling plot. It also had excellent mod support, including the FreeSpace Editor (FRED) for mission creation. The game's alternate title, "Descent: FreeSpace – The Great War", has little connection to the separate Descent series of games.
Story of FreeSpace 1:
Terrans and Vasudans are locked in a 14-year war with billions of casualties. A new enemy, the Shivans, emerges, attacking both sides and forcing them to unite to save their civilizations.

Silent Threat Expansion:
Silent Threat, an expansion for FreeSpace 1 released later in 1998, features a new campaign about the Hades Rebellion, a rogue faction within the GTA. It introduces new ships, weapons, command briefings, and fan-created missions. The expansion was considered decent.
Story of Silent Threat:
During the Great War with the Shivans, the player is commissioned by Galactic Terran Intelligence (GTI) for special operations to preserve the fragile Terran-Vasudan peace treaty, navigating possible deception by the GTI itself amid the wider war with the Shivans.

FreeSpace 2:
Released in 1999, FreeSpace 2 (FS2) continues the story 32 years after FS1. Terrans and Vasudans increased their cooperation and formed the Galactic Terran-Vasudan Alliance (GTVA). However, the Neo-Terran Front arises and rebels against the GTVA, opposing the alliance with the Vasudans. FS2 improved upon FS1 with new features such as beam weapons for capital ships, new ships like the corvettes, missions in nebula environments and additional fighters and weapons for the player.

Universe of FreeSpace:
The FreeSpace universe continues human history as they interact with two alien species. Canon events span 2335 to 2367, focusing on these dates. In 2335, the Terrans (humans) and Vasudans had been at war for fourteen years when the technologically superior Shivans appeared. The two species united to combat the Shivans, known as the Great War (chronicled in FS1). The Shivans were defeated, but the Vasudan homeworld was destroyed, and contact with Earth was lost.
Shortly after FS1, the Hades Rebellion occurred. Rogue elements of Galactic Terran Intelligence built the GTD Hades with Shivan weaponry. This rebellion ended with the Hades's destruction, documented in the Silent Threat expansion.
The Reconstruction period (2335-2367) saw the Terrans and Vasudans rebuilding and forming the Galactic Terran-Vasudan Alliance (GTVA). Eighteen months before FreeSpace 2, Admiral Aken Bosch led a military coup - the Neo-Terran Front (NTF), threatening the GTVA.
FS 2 covers the final weeks of the NTF rebellion and the Shivans' return, known as the Second Shivan Incursion or Second Great War. The NTF is defeated, but the Shivans gather a powerful fleet in the Capella system. The GTVA destroys the subspace nodes leading out of Capella, isolating the Shivan fleet.
The era after this, with no canon events, is called the Post-Capella Era by the community.
The FreeSpace galaxy has numerous star systems, navigable through subspace using fixed-position jump nodes. The time required to travel through subspace is unclear; the last mission of FS1 suggests a 10-minute window to complete the mission before the SD Lucifer enters Sol through the Delta Serpentis-Sol jump node.

Races and Factions of FreeSpace:

Terrans:
Terrans (humans) originate from Earth (Terra) in the Sol (solar) system. Though physically weak, they are highly intelligent and versatile, evolving from nomadic primates to a spacefaring race. The Terran civilization developed stable nation-states that eventually unified into the Galactic Terran Alliance (GTA). Terrans have colonized twelve worlds outside Sol and established outposts on fifteen others. The Galactic Terran Intelligence (GTI) is the GTA's intelligence arm, responsible for intelligence gathering, strategic planning and research and development of new military technologies used against alliance's adversaries. Special Operations Command (SOC) is closely related to Galactic Terran Intelligence and serves as GTI's combat arm.

Vasudans:
Vasudans are tall, skeletal-looking bipeds with powerful musculature from Vasuda Prime, a harsh desert planet in the Vasuda system. Both of the twin continents of Vasuda Prime are almost entirely desert, and most of the above-ground water is undrinkable. Their society and culture is complex, with numerous social tests and protocols. Misunderstanding these protocols, such as The Conversation, contributed to the beginning of the Terran-Vasudan War. The Vasudan government, the Parliamentary Vasudan Empire (PVE), is a parliamentary system with an Imperial family and Ambassadors, who are quasi-royal go-betweens between the Parliament and the Imperial family. The Parliamentary Vasudan Navy (PVN) is the space military branch. Driven by resource scarcity, Vasudans intensified space exploration to find habitable planets.

Hammer of Light:
The Hammer of Light (HoL) is a hostile Vasudan cult that worships the Shivans, viewing them as cosmic destroyers and preservers. Active during the Great War, they believe in ancient prophecies foretelling the Shivans' arrival.

Shivans:
Shivans are an enigmatic species whose origins and motives remain unknown. They have multiple eyes, five legs and can run equally well over a floor or over a ceiling, suggesting an evolution in a zero-gravity environment. Shivans possess strong claws and strange thoracic compartments. They may be robots or organic beings in exo-skeletons, but such hypotheses are mostly considered only unfounded speculations. All communication attempts with Shivans have failed, so we still do not know why they seem focused on the destruction of all other sentient species. Most likely, we will not learn anything more about the Shivan species until we have actually captured and studied a Shivan.

Ancients:
The Ancients, now extinct, were an advanced species with an extensive interstellar empire, wiped out by the Shivans around 8,000 years ago. Their knowledge of subspace tracking, discovered in ruins in the Altair system, enabled the destruction of the Lucifer. The Ancients also built the Knossos portals that provided a link between Shivan and GTVA-held space, which allowed the Second Shivan Incursion to occur in 2367. Evidence suggests a relationship between the Ancients and primitive Vasudans, as their languages are similar, and Vasudan legends seemingly reference the Ancients as their ancestors. The Hammer of Light's prophecies about cosmic destroyers may also stem from Ancients' warnings about the Shivans.

Subspace:
Subspace is an n-dimensional tunnel between one point in the universe and another. A vessel equipped with a subspace jump drive can travel through this tunnel in a matter of minutes, making a journey that might otherwise take decades or even centuries at light speed. A subspace jump drive synchronizes a ship's vibrations with the subspace continuum, creating a vortex that can be traversed. There are two types of subspace jumps: intrasystem jump (within a single star system) and intersystem jump (between star systems).
Intrasystem Jumps: Both large and small vessels use these to travel short distances within a star system. The presence of an intense gravitational field is required, prohibiting travel beyond the boundaries of a star system.
Intersystem Jumps: Some ships use intersystem subspace jump nodes for travel between star systems. Initially, only large ships could generate sufficient power to make these jumps, but in 2335, the development of small intersystem jump drives for fighters and bombers allowed for critical missions, like the destruction of the SD Lucifer. These small intersystem jump drives are expensive and used sparingly.
Most subspace nodes are unstable and short-lived, but some remain stable for centuries and are strategically important locations. Jump drives require time to recharge. This process can take many minutes. Larger ships can't jump near intersystem jump nodes, necessitating sublight travel to and from these nodes. This is why pilots often escort ships to and from a node or are tasked with preventing enemy ships from reaching a node.

Timeline:
    2300-2321: Unification Era
		Sometime during this period, Terrans discovered Subspace, unified under the Galactic Terran Alliance (GTA), explored the nearest star systems and set up colonies in several systems. This era covers the period from before the discovery of subspace to the first contact with the Vasudans.
    2321-2335: The 14-Year War (Terran-Vasudan War)
		Shortly after the first contact, the Terran-Vasudan War, or the 14-Year War, broke out. Heavy and sustained casualties were suffered on both sides, but both species learned valuable lessons in adaptation and the development of new technologies. This period spans from the first contact with the Vasudans to the signing of the treaty that officially ended the 14-Year War.
    2335: The Great War (First Shivan Incursion)
		The Great War was the first invasion of the menacing Shivans in the year 2335. The outbreak of this war caused Terrans and Vasudans to set aside their differences, sign a peace treaty and combat the mutual threat of the Shivans. The Shivan armada, led by the powerful superdestroyer SD Lucifer with impervious shields, marched through the Terran and Vasudan lines of defense, destroyed the Vasudan homeworld of Vasuda Prime and then set a course for Earth. Ultimately, Ancient artifacts discovered in Deneb and Altair led to the Lucifer's destruction while exiting the jump node to Sol. The jump node was collapsed by the events, severing Earth from the subspace network.
    2335: The Hades Rebellion
		The Hades Rebellion was an attempted coup initiated by rogue elements of Galactic Terran Intelligence following the events of the Great War. It was cataloged in the Silent Threat campaign.
    2335-2366: Reconstruction Era
		The Reconstruction Era was a series of political and historical events which led to advancement of Terran and Vasudan technology and preparation for the threat of another Shivan incursion. This era included many major events, such as the formation of the Galactic Terran-Vasudan Alliance (GTVA) and signing of the Beta Aquilae Convention (BETAC).
    2366-2367: The NTF Rebellion
		The NTF Rebellion was an open rebellion against the Galactic Terran-Vasudan Alliance by Admiral Aken Bosch and his loyalists in the Neo-Terran Front.
    2367: The Second Shivan Incursion
		The Second Shivan Incursion was the second invasion of Terran-Vasudan space by the Shivans.
    2367-2400: Post-Capella Era
		 The Post-Capella period is the era which occurs after the Destruction of Capella.

Node Inconsistencies:
There are some jump node connections that are apparently used in FreeSpace lore, but are not illustrated on the official Freespace jump node map.
	Deneb - Altair
		Used by the player's squadron and Omega wing of transports in Act 3. The node is seen in Reaching the Zenith (Act 3 mission 4) and used in Running the Gauntlet (Act 3 mission 5) and Black Omega (Act 3 mission 6). Xenolinguists are transported from Deneb to Altair to retrieve the ancient records and then back to Deneb to rejoin the Terran fleet.
	Sirius - Delta Serpentis
		Used by the SD Lucifer and GTD Bastion in Clash of the Titans (Act 3 mission 7). The Bastion unsuccessfully tries to intercept the SD Lucifer before it can jump from Sirius to Delta Serpentis. Lucifer is on the way to attack Sol.
	Ribos - Beta Cygni
		This jump corridor may have been alluded to in Paving the Way (Act 1 mission 6), when Admiral Wolf says that standard procedure would have the Galatea circumvent the Antares-Beta Cygni jump corridor and make two jumps. Jumping from Antares to Betelgeuse and then to Beta Cygni would satisfy this requirement, but so would Antares to Ribos and then to Beta Cygni, through the possible Ribos-Beta Cygni corridor.
It is likely that these jump nodes really exist, but are simply too unstable to be safely used under normal circumstances, hence are missing from the official node map. But in extraordinary or desperate circumstances, even such unstable jump nodes are sometimes used by military ships. Also, Shivan subspace jump drives are more efficient than Terran and Vasudan ones, so they can effectively use jump nodes that are hardly usable for Terran and Vasudan ships - this can explain the Lucifer jumping from Sirius to Delta Serpentis.

Ship size classes (from smallest to largest, with average length specified):
1. Fighters (20 meters)
2. Bombers (40 meters)
3. Cruisers (250 meters)
4. Corvettes - introduced in Freespace 2 (750 meters)
5. Destroyers (2000 meters)
6. Superdestroyers or Juggernauts (3000-6000 meters)

Destroyers and superdestroyers/juggernauts are called Capital Ships. Fleets are always led by a Capital Ship. Only corvettes, destroyers, superdestroyers and installations (stations) have fighterbays capable of holding fighters and bombers (cruisers cannot carry fighters/bombers). Fighters and bombers are typically deployed in wings containing from 3 to 6 ships.
All ships have destroyable subsystems, such as engines, weapons, navigation, sensors and communications. Larger ships also have turrets and dockpoints.

Terran spacecraft classes

	Fighters (prefix GTF)
		GTF Apollo
			The GTF Apollo is the most common GTA fighter. It is highly versatile and can perform multiple roles, including space superiority, interception and assault.
		GTF Ulysses
			Affectionately nicknamed The Bat by its pilots, the GTF Ulysses is a well-designed craft that has had a far longer useful life than its engineers ever imagined. Developed jointly by Terran and Vasudan contractors, the Ulysses was first deployed near the end of the Great War. Its excellent maneuverability and high top speed give it an agility that pilots love, but at a cost of thin armor and a small secondary weapon loadout.
		GTF Valkyrie
			Using the old GTF Angel Scout Fighter as a template, the GTA created the Valkyrie Interceptor Fighter. It has two additional engines, as well as an additional set of primary hardpoints. This makes the Valkyrie the best bomber interceptor in the fleet. Its speed is unmatched in the GTA, and its maneuverability is above average. Its only drawbacks are a small secondary payload and weak hull.
		GTF Hercules
			The GTF Hercules is the slowest and most unmanageable fighter in the fleet. This is offset, however, by its overpowering weaponry and shielding. It carries 6 primary weapons, as well as two full secondary missile banks. This heavy assault fighter is best suited for assault missions and bomber escort.
		GTF Loki
			Designed by GTI engineers, the GTF Loki is undoubtedly the best reconaissance craft in Terran or Vasudan service. Its hull shape and shielded reactor chamber lends itself to providing a low sensor profile, and is yet capable of very high speed maneuvers. However, its relatively light armament and low armor rating make it unsuitable for extended firefights.
	
	Bombers (prefix GTB)
		GTB Athena
			The smallest bomber in the fleet, the Athena has oftentimes been mistaken for a fighter. However, one should not be fooled by its small size. The Athena can carry light bombs, and most missiles. It also has the ability to dogfight well. Its strong shielding and hull make it the favored "Lightning Bomber" of the GTA.
		GTB Medusa
			The Medusa is the standard attack bomber in the GTA. Its strong shielding and large secondary capacity make it the favored attack craft against cruiser class targets. As the first bomber to carry the Tsunami bomb, the Medusa is considered the staple of any bomber pilot's career.
		GTB Ursa
			The Ursa is the first bomber designed specifically for the purpose of annihilating capital ships. It carries a massive payload and is the only bomber in the GTA which can equip the Harbinger Bomb. Strong shielding and hull make the Ursa very difficult to destroy.
		GTB Zeus
			Another product of GTI R&D, the Zeus strike bomber is a worthy successor to the Athena. Its balanced design and increased reactor capacity give it remarkable speed for a bomber, yet it still remains capable of carrying the powerful Tsunami bombs. These features will make the Zeus a versatile and effective weapon against capital ships.
	
	Transports (prefix GTT)
		GTT Elysium
			Since the start of space colonization, this standard transport has been used by everyone, both civilian and military. While it has undergone minor changes time and again, it remains a simple design: A vehicle meant to transport personnel from one place to another.
		GTT Hunter
			The GTT Hunter was designed by GTI for surgical strike purposes requiring small squads of highly-trained marines.  Faster, smaller, and more heavily armed than the Elysium, it is capable of performing limited offensive roles in addition to carrying soldiers and cargo.  The GTI primarily uses these ships to deposit small teams of special forces units to soften up the enemy in preparation for larger assaults by conventional boarding parties.
	
	Freighters (prefix GTFr)
		GTFr Chronos
			The Chronos is a freighter of civilian design. As the Terran Vasudan war dragged on, more and more of these vessels were commissioned by the GTA for use in military operations. These vessels are slow but possess relatively strong hull.
		GTFr Poseidon
			The Poseidon is the standard military freighter in the GTA. It is designed to protect its cargo from any kind of attack, and has four turrets with which to do so.
	
	Cruisers (prefix GTC)
		GTC Fenris
			The mainstay of the Terran Fleet, these vessels have served in both strike and defense purposes. From nose to tail it measures 260 meters. With a full array of weapon systems and a strong enough hull to withstand the strongest enemy warheads, a Fenris Cruiser can be found in almost any system that the GTA is operating in. The Fenris cruiser was originally designed as a strike weapon, hence its fast speed and decent turning rate. It was later decided that a second line of cruisers would be produced, for defensive purposes, once it became apparent that the Terran-Vasudan war wouldn't be over in a few months.
		GTC Leviathan
			After the Vasudans began making incursions into GTA space, command decided that it needed a new defensive cruiser. Changes to the Fenris led to the GTC Leviathan line of cruisers, produced as mobile defense battleships. Their speed and maneuverability were greatly reduced in tradeoff for more powerful weapons and a stronger hull. Production was discontinued when the GTA thought they would win the Vasudan war after the Battle of Gulnara, and then the production was started up again after the defeat at the Talania system. Because of the on-again off-again nature of this vessel's production, almost all of the Leviathan Cruisers have different armaments, but all have consistent hulls and speeds.
	
	Destroyers (prefix GTD)
		GTD Orion
			The Orion is the capital ship of the GTA. Measuring a frightening 2.1 kilometers in length, the cost to build one of these far outweighs the cost of paying the crew of this ship for 3 years. There is no more important symbol of Terran Pride than a ship like the Galatea or the Intrepid cruising past a colonized planet, patrolling the system and ensuring safety. In the course of the 14 year war, very few of these have ever been lost, making the destruction of an Orion a truly horrible defeat.
	
	Juggernauts (prefix GTD)
		GTD Hades
			The GTD Hades was a fusion of Terran and Shivan military technologies. It was constructed by Galactic Terran Intelligence (GTI), a rogue branch of the GTA. In 2335, GTI leaders sought to overthrow the provisional GTA government established in Delta Serpentis. After the defeat of the GTI and the destruction of the Hades, the GTA classified the details of this research at level Omega. There are rumors that the Alliance has attempted to reconstruct the Hades to learn more about the GTI's activities. These reports have yet to be confirmed by government sources.
	
	Installations (prefix GTI)
		GTI Arcadia
			Early Terran space stations were constructed much smaller, and were mostly used for Zero-G research purposes. Later on, with advancements in space construction technologies, stations grew bigger and bigger. When the first Arcadia-class station was commissioned, GTA decided to rename it to an Installation rather than a Space Station. Used for a variety of purposes, almost all major systems are home to at least one Installation, which is often used as the center of trade and communications. Home to scores of small ship, including repair vessels, fighters, and transports, an Arcadia class Installation is always a safe haven for a convoy of freighters or ships returning from battle. Where the Orion is the symbol of Terran power, the Arcadia is the shining beacon of stability.
		Terran Asteroid Base
			Asteroid bases such as these are used by both civilian and military contractors to mine resources and conduct experiments.
	
	Science Vessels (prefix GTSC)
		GTSC Faustus
			The Faustus Science vessel was, and still is, a civilian craft. However, in the 14 years since the Terran-Vasudan war began, more and more of these were commissioned by the GTA in order to pursue military research. However, its obvious value, poor fighting capabilities, and lack of speed make it a prime target for enemy ships; hence, you will rarely find many of these anywhere near the front lines of battle.
		Terran Probe
			The standard Terran Probe is an all-purpose instrument that can be used in a variety of situations.  They are found in missions ranging from unmanned exploration of new systems, to scientific observation of interesting areas of space, to surveying of patrol routes or suspected enemy positions.  In a pinch, they can be deployed as communications buoys or as couriers for important messages or scientific data.  Their great speed and small size make them useful in battle situations where the enemy will most probably give them a low target priority.
	
	Escape Pods (prefix GTEP)
		GTEP Hermes
			The Hermes Escape Pod has been used since the destruction of the GTD Goliath over 12 years ago. They are used by the crews of destroyers and installations to escape destruction. It is always best to be prepared.
	
	Support Ships (prefix GTS)
		GTS Centaur
			The Centaur support ship is designed to get in and out of battle as quickly as possible. It is fast and quite maneuverable, allowing it to dock with fighters in a minimum amount of time. Most notable about the Centaur, however, is its extremely large explosion radius. The amount of warheads carried on a support ship makes it very dangerous to be close to an exploding Centaur.
	
	Training Drones (prefix GTDr)
		GTDr Amazon
			The Amazon is a simple and cheap unmanned drone, used for basic combat training. They carry only the weakest training weaponry.
		GTDr Amazon Advanced
			The Advanced Amazon is a larger (but still inexpensive) unmanned drone, used by the GTA for subsystem and large target training.
	
	Navigation Buoys (no prefix)
		Terran NavBuoy
			An unmanned navigation buoy.

	Sentry Guns (prefix GTSG)
		GTSG Watchdog
			The Watchdog is the standard unmanned defensive sentry turret for the GTA. It fires two standard plasma bursts at any nearby hostile targets.
		GTSG Cerberus
			The Cerberus is an advanced version of the Watchdog, with stronger weaponry and the ability to withstand more damage.
	
	Cargo Containers (no prefix)
		TC 2
			The TC2 is the standard issue cargo container in the GTA for deep space cargo transport. It has all the electronics and systems needed to preserve any cargo within it at whatever conditions are necessary. TC2s are cheap to manufacture, and heavily used throughout the Alliance.
		TSC 2
			The TSC 2 was developed when Terran Command decided it needed a stronger and more secure cargo container for use in military operations.  It improves on the established TC 2 design by sacrificing a small amount of internal space for a significantly stronger hull.  Although it is still not as strong as the TAC 1, the TSC 2 is usually the GTA's container of choice for cargo with military value.
		TAC 1
			The TAC 1 is a heavily armored container, usually for carrying volatile or dangerous substances. It is also the preferred supply container for use in front line operations.
		TTC 1
			The TTC 1 is another example of civilian equipment used by the GTA military. It is primarily a Tech Container, used for research purposes, especially Zero-G development operations. It is not very strong, however, and is a priority target of enemy attacks.

Vasudan spacecraft classes

	Fighters (prefix PVF)
		PVF Anubis
			The Anubis is the weakest fighter in the Vasudan Navy. However, they are extremely cheap to manufacture, and are often used in swarm situations to try to overwhelm the opponent with numbers. A few radical Vasudans have been known to load the ship with explosives and attempt to steer themselves into GTA capital ships. The first occurrence of this was the battle of Rexias 4, where the GTD Goliath was destroyed by a squadron of kamikaze pilots.
		PVF Horus
			The Horus interceptor can outrun any ship in the PVN or the GTA. This makes it extremely dangerous. Its above average weapons capacity, combined with its good maneuverability and shielding make this one of the most well designed Interceptors in the galaxy. This ship is responsible for the destruction of more GTA bombers than any other.
		PVF Seth
			As the slowest fighter in the PVN, the Seth has earned itself a reputation as the Turtle of the Vasudan fleet. Even though it is quite compact in size, the Seth can carry a massive payload, and has a lot of shielding. Its standard impulse engines aren't very powerful, but the Seth gains an incredible speed boost when using its afterburners. The Seth is primarily used is convoy attacks and capital ship escort duties.
		PVF Thoth
			The Thoth is the most dangerous fighter to be created by the PVN. Manufactured solely at the Altairian Ship Yards, the Thoth has only been produced in small numbers as of yet. When the Altairian Yards defected to the Hammer of Light rebels, the production of Thoths by the PVN dropped to nil. The Thoth is now completely in the hands of the Hammer of Light.
	
	Bombers (prefix PVB)
		PVB Amun
			The Amun is the Vasudans' heaviest bomber class ship. It carries a massive payload and has been responsible for the destruction of 3 Orion-class destroyers in the past 2 years. Fortunately, it is slow and has low maneuverability, making it an easy target for our fighters. Fighter pilots should be wary of the two turrets on this ship: they are not to be ignored.
		PVB Osiris
			The Osiris has now become the standard bomber for use in PVN operations. It has replaced the Amun, correcting many of the faults of its predecessor. It is not quite as sturdy, but it has nearly the same weapons capacity, and is faster and more maneuverable.
		PVB Sekhmet
			The PVB Sekhmet is a Hammer of Light ship that appears to be a fusion of Vasudan and Shivan technology.  The Hammer of Light claims that its design was adapted from a new class of Shivan bomber heretofore unencountered by either the GTA or the PVN. Sporting four primary weapon hardpoints and three missile bays, the Sekhmet packs a massive punch.  Treat it as a class A threat to all allied capital ships.
	
	Transports (prefix PVT)
		PVT Isis
			The Isis transport is quite fast and maneuverable for a ship of its size. Most often it is used for personnel transfer, although occasionally they have been known to act as repair ships and tugboats. It is well protected with its turrets and it is not unheard of for the PVN to use a wing of Isises to conduct an assault on a fortified position.
	
	Freighters (prefix PVFr)
		PVFr Bast
			The Bast freighter is unarmed, and considered a non threat. We believe that it is mainly used by Vasudan civilians, but we have seen some in front line operations. It has very low target value, but should be destroyed if encountered at the front, as any kind of resupply ship is a danger to the GTA.
		PVFr Ma'at
			With three turrets, the Ma'at is a hairy target for GTA pilots to attack. However, it is slow enough that it can be destroyed with enough patience. As always, supply ships for the PVN are considered to be valid targets.
		PVFr Satis
			The dreaded Satis freighter was thought to be a warship when it was first encountered in Aldebaran. It has five turrets and a strong hull, making it quite dangerous to the inexperienced pilot. However, most experienced pilots in the GTA know enough to attack the Satis' main weakness: its weak plating around the turrets. Disarming a Satis is most often the best way to destroy it.
	
	Cruisers (prefix PVC)
		PVC Aten
			The Aten class cruiser, while far stronger than most of the Vasudans warships, falls short as a attack cruiser. It does not have the armor or the firepower to stand up to GTA weaponry. With a cruiser speed of 25 m/s and only six weapon turrets, the Aten just cannot muster the kind of firepower needed to do real damage to targets of size. Most GTA pilots have learned to exploit the primary weakness of the Aten: its extremely weak weapons subsystem. The Aten class cruiser is considered by the GTA to be a Class B threat.
	
	Destroyers (prefix PVD)
		PVD Typhon
			Where the Aten cruiser failed, the Typhon succeeded well past expectations. The Typhon is an incredible work of engineering and the model of Vasudan technology. When the first one was sighted at the Vega Engagement, it was laughed at by our technicians as a foolish display of non-utilitarian design. The subsequent destruction of the GTD Eisenhower and the total decimation of the 4th fleet changed their minds very quickly. The Typhon should never be underestimated. It wields massive weaponry, and has more armor plating than any Terran Destroyer. Its only known weakness is its turret armor, which is significant, but lower than expected. In the two years since this ship first appeared, we have only managed to destroy two of them. The Typhon is considered a Class A threat to any ships within the same system. Do not engage without backup.
	
	Installations (prefix PVI)
		PVI Karnak
			The gargantuan Karnak installation is the Vasudans' answer to the GTI Arcadia. This monstrous facility bristles with dozens of turrets and can house up to two full squadrons of fighters. Its primary purpose is to serve as a construction and maintenance yard for the Vasudan fleets. Because of its enormous logistical and strategic value, the Karnak is considered a class A threat.
		Vasudan Asteroid Base
			Asteroid bases such as these are used by both civilian and military contractors to mine resources and conduct experiments.
	
	Science Vessels (prefix PVSC)
		PVSC Imhotep
			The PVSC Imhotep is the standard Vasudan research vessel.  Like our GTSC Faustus, the Imhotep can fill a variety of scientific roles, including performing on-board research and conducting sensor surveys.  One major difference between the two classes is that the Imhotep carries a small podbay housing four remote research pods.  These pods are frequently launched to augment the Imhotep's on-board systems.
		Vasudan Probe
			This large probe is more of a fully-fledged research pod than a simple drone.  Designed to work in collaboration with an Imhotep science cruiser or as part of team of probes, it is capable of numerous roles including observation, experimentation, and scientific analysis.  It is often possible for a wing of probes to conduct minor missions independent of outside supervision, allowing Vasudan Command to reserve their Imhoteps for deployment where the personal presence of scientists is most needed.
	
	Escape Pods (prefix PVEP)
		PVEP Ra
			The Ra has been used by Vasudans since our first encounter with them. Every Vasudan capital ship or installation has a few of these lifeboats.
	
	Support Ships (prefix PVS)
		PVS Scarab
			The Scarab is very similar to our Centaur. It is used for in flight rearming of fighters and bombers. In longer engagements, these ships can mean the difference between victory and defeat. The major difference between this ship and the Centaur is that the Scarab is unmanned.
	
	Training Drones (prefix PVDr)
		PVDr Jackal
			The Jackal is a simple training and reconnaissance drone used by the PVN.  As the Jackal could be mass-produced quickly and cheaply, it was not long before the Vasudans used it as a template for the PVF Anubis, a piloted variant well suited for use as a swarm fighter. The Jackal has never been used offensively against the GTA and is classified as a non-threat.
	
	Navigation Buoys (no prefix)

	Sentry Guns (prefix PVSG)
		PVSG Ankh
			The Ankh is the standard Vasudan sentry gun. Its most common usage is guarding supply depots.
	
	Cargo Containers (no prefix)
		VC 3

Vasudans used standardized cargo containers well before we did, as they needed to transport raw materials from other systems to their own barren world. Our cargo container was modelled after theirs, although ours is slightly superior in durability.
		VAC 4
			the VAC 4 series of cargo is much like its normal counterpart, only it has much more armor plating. However, this container not dangerous in itself and is nothing more than ML-16 laser bait without protection of some kind.

Shivan spacecraft classes

	Fighters (prefix SF)
		SF Scorpion
			We originally thought the Scorpion was the Shivans' best fighter. However, we now believe that the Scorpion is nothing more than a Shivan scout ship. Their vast numbers and seeming unimportance to the Shivan war effort leads us to this belief. Regardless of its purpose, the Scorpion is highly maneuverable, fast, and difficult to destroy. It can also do damage equivalent to our space superiority fighters.
		SF Basilisk
			The Basilisk seems to be a Shivan heavy attack fighter. It is not as maneuverable as other Shivan fighters, but it packs quite a punch, and it is very resistant to our weaponry. Most often they have been used in attacking our supply convoys and transports.
		SF Manticore
			We believe that the Manticore is a Shivan interceptor. It has a very high top speed, and its maneuverability is quite good. However, its shield system isn't strong enough to withstand constant fighting for long, which leads us to believe the Manticore's primary purpose is to destroy enemy bombers.
		SF Dragon
			The Shivan Dragon is easily the most impressive fightercraft we have yet seen. Its maneuverability is unmatched, and it has quite a few other advantages, including a good top speed, powerful afterburners, and strong shields. It is the only Shivan fighter we have been able to study up close, and we have learned a great deal about Shivan engineering from it. It seems that the Shivan shield systems are extremely strong, but their hulls are weaker than our own. They also rely on primary weapons far more then secondaries, as evidenced by the Dragon's small missile capacity.
		SF Gorgon
			The first encounter with the SF Gorgon was made by the GTD Saratoga in the Ikeya system.  We are uncertain if this is a new Shivan design or if the Shivans have simply held this particular class of ship back for the majority of the war.  The ship's unusual design not only provides it with exceptional maneuverability, but also allows it to generate an extremely powerful shield.  So far these ships have only been sighted leading wings of lesser fighters, but there have been reports of entire wings of Gorgons in larger forces. Little else is known about the Gorgon.  It should be considered a class B threat when acting as a wing leader, but a full wing of Gorgons is to be treated as a class A threat to all allied vessels.
	
	Bombers (prefix SB)
		SB Shaitan
			The Shaitan bomber is only slightly superior to our own bombers - its shielding is better, but its payload capacity seems to be far worse. Perhaps the Shivans were so assured that they would only need one payload bay to destroy a target.
		SB Nephilim
			The Nephilim class bomber is the most alien of the Shivan ships. We are still unsure as to why the Shivans chose this odd shape for arguably their most powerful attack craft. However, with four primary cannons and two turrets, as well as a massive payload, the Nephilim is undoubtedly a serious threat to the GTA. We have already lost two Orion class destroyers to a wing of Nephilims. Unless we learn a weakness soon, we will most likely lose more. The Nephilim has been designated a Class A threat to GTA destroyers and cruisers.
		SB Seraphim
			The newly encountered Seraphim class bomber is the Shivans' latest threat. It is three times larger than a Nephilim and carries a massive payload at least 150% the size of the Nephilim. With strong hulls and almost impenetrable shield system, the Seraphim is easily the most dangerous bomber we have yet encountered. The Seraphim has been designated a Class A threat to all GTA destroyers and cruisers.
		SB Nahema
			The SB Nahema has never actually been encountered by either GTA or PVN forces, as far as we can determine.  What little intelligence we have on this bomber comes from our interrogations of HOL prisoners and is strictly classified by GTI.  We do know that the Nahema was the base from which the HOL constructed the PVB Sekhmet, their Vasudan-Shivan hybrid prototype bomber.  How the HOL managed to acquire scans of the Nahema in the first place is unknown.
	
	Transports (prefix ST)
		ST Azrael
			The Azrael is most likely a Shivan transport. While we are not completely sure of its uses, we do know that they can house many Shivans. It is not very heavily armored, and its weaponry is not too strong. This makes the Azrael the most common target for capture attempts.
	
	Freighters (prefix SFr)
		SFr Mephisto
			The Mephisto freighter behaves much like one of our own, only it is far more powerful and dangerous. Very little data is available on this ship class.
		SFr Asmodeus
			Almost no data is available on the Asmodeus freighter. We are aware that it has a strong hull and four turrets. Any further data on this ship should be immediately delivered to Terran Intelligence.
	
	Cruisers (prefix SC)
		SC Cain
			The Cain is by far the most common cruiser in the Shivan fleet. It is also the weakest. However, the Cain should not be underestimated. It has many turrets, as well as a cluster bomb defensive mechanism. As a strike cruiser, the Cain mostly appears in attacks against GTA or PVN capital ships and cruisers. In these cases, it should be destroyed immediately. The primary weakness of the Cain appears to be its unshielded turrets. Most of the turrets on the Cain can be destroyed with minimal damage. The Cain is a Class B threat.
		SC Lilith
			While far more rare than its counterpart (Cain-class cruiser), the Lilith is one of the most ferocious cruisers we have had the misfortune of combating. It has extremely strong weapon systems, as well as a very thick armor which our cannons can barely penetrate. One on one, the Lilith can easily destroy any of our cruisers. It is for this reason that they should be considered a primary target for all bomber squadrons in the GTA. The Lilith should be considered a Class A threat to any ships that encounter it.
	
	Destroyers (prefix SD)
		SD Demon
			While the Demon-class Destroyer is easily one of the most massive ships we have yet seen, it is not the biggest or most powerful in the Shivan armada - that honor is reserved for their Superdestroyers. However, the Demon is still quite dangerous, with a plethora of missile turrets and two full squadrons of fighters and bombers. It is suggested that these capital ships should be destroyed by bombers, as all cruisers that have attempted to engage a Demon have been destroyed. The Demon is a Class A threat in any allied system.
	
	Superdestroyers (prefix SD)
		SD Lucifer
			The Lucifer is the greatest threat to the survival of the GTA, the PVN, and both the Terran and Vasudan species. It wields three massive Flux Cannons which can destroy one of our capital ships in a few hits. These same cannons have been seen bombarding colonized worlds. With four full fighter squadrons, and a massive array of defensive turrets, the Lucifer will be extremely difficult to destroy.
			The fact that it is protected by a shielding system which makes it completely impervious to any kind of kinetic or plasma damage makes it impossible to destroy. We have yet to find a way to breach this shielding technology. It is hoped that a solution will be found soon. Assuming that a solution is found, we have managed to gather enough data on the Lucifer to destroy it. In a recon mission, we were able to determine that the Lucifer is powered by five reactors across its surface. If these reactors are destroyed in a short amount of time, the Lucifer will be stopped.
			If we cannot stop the Lucifer, we do not expect to be able to defeat the Shivans.
			We have no way of knowing if there are more than one Lucifer-class destroyers. However, any that exist should be considered more dangerous than a Class A threat.
	
	Sentry Guns (prefix SSG)
		SSG Trident
			We believe that the main purpose of the Trident is to guard Shivan repair and supply depots. It is not too strong, but can be dangerous in mass quantities.
	
	Cargo Containers (no prefix)
		SC 5
			Our scientists believe that the Shivans use cargo containers such as these as storage depots. We have yet to see a Shivan land on a planet. This leads us to believe that the Shivans plan on keeping all of their materials and resources in deep space, stored in containers like these.
		SAC 2
			What we have dubbed the SAC 2 is simply a more heavily armored form of the standard Shivan cargo container. We believe that this is where the Shivans keep their more precious supplies. SAC 2 containers are to be captured or destroyed at every opportunity.

Terran Weaponry

1. Primary Weapons (Guns)
	GTW ML-16 Laser: High rate of fire, low hull damage, negligible shield penetration. Can be equipped by every fighter/bomber.
	GTW-15 Avenger: Good hull damage, decent shield suppression. Compatible with every fighter/bomber.
	GTW Flail: High velocity and rate of fire, low damage per shot, decent against shields. Has kinetic effects on target. Cannot be equipped by GFT Valkyrie and GTB Medusa.
	GTW Prometheus: The ultimate hull killer, also pretty good against shields. High power consumption. Cannot be equipped by GTB Athena.
	GTW Banshee: The ultimate shield killer. Low hull damage. High power consumption. Low compatibility: can only be equipped by GTF Valkyrie, GTF Hercules and GTF Loki.
	GTW Disruptor / GTM D-Advanced: Low hull damage, high subsystem damage. Useful for disabling engines or destroying turrets on capital ships without killing the ship. Disruptor can be equipped by every fighter/bomber except the GTB Medusa. D-Advanced is experimental and only compatible with GTF Ulysses.

2. Secondary Weapons (Missiles)
	GTM MX-50: Heat-seeking. Weak damage, weak tracking.
	GTM Fury: Small, fast dumbfire missiles, low yield, high rate of fire. Use in close combat situations.
	GTM Interceptor: Aspect-seeking, high velocity, pretty high damage.
	GTM Hornet: Aspect-seeking swarm (4 missiles per volley). High hull damage, if all 4 missiles hit the target.
	GTM Phoenix V: Aspect-seeking, High yield, relatively slow velocity, low rate of fire. Anti-bomber (interception) missile.

FS1 campaign synopsis

The player serves as a GTA pilot aboard the GTD Galatea. The campaign begins with routine anti-Vasudan patrols and raids in the closing phase of the 14-Year War, including fighting Terran defectors and contesting systems such as Antares and Beta Cygni. This abruptly changes when the enigmatic Shivans appear, possessing ships with sensor-cloaking technology and powerful shielding that makes them impervious to Terran and Vasudan weapons. They start annihilating both Terran and Vasudan forces, forcing the two powers into an uneasy ceasefire. The new war is complicated by the Hammer of Light, a rogue Vasudan cult that worships the Shivans, rejects the ceasefire and actively aids the invasion.

As the conflict escalates, the alliance learns how to track Shivans on radar, develops new weapons that punch through their shields, then recovers and adapts Shivan shielding technology. In a daring raid, pilots from the Galatea attempt to capture the Shivan cruiser Taranis. The capture of the cruiser proves disastrous when the Shivans track it through subspace and the Lucifer superdestroyer (protected by new, invulnerable shields) destroys Tombaugh Station in Ribos to erase the prize. The Shivans continue a rapid jump-node offensive, breaking allied defenses across Ribos, Antares, Beta Aquilae, and Deneb. After GTD Galatea is destroyed by the Lucifer, the player transfers to GTD Bastion. The Lucifer bypasses blockades through unstable or uncharted routes, and Vasuda Prime is bombarded into ruin. Late in the war, the alliance also fields major emergency advances including improved anti-Shivan weapons, Tsunami and Harbinger bombs, Ursa bombers, and a limited number of fighter-borne intersystem jump drives.

A desperate chance emerges when a Vasudan refugee fleet in Altair system discovers the ruins of a long-extinct civilisation on an uncharted world. These "Ancients" once ruled a vast empire before the Shivans destroyed them. Their records reveal two critical flaws: Shivan capital ships can be tracked in subspace, and the Lucifer's shields will not function there. After covering evacuations, escorting the retrieval of the Altair records, and fighting rearguard actions around Alpha Centauri, Sirius, and Delta Serpentis, the Bastion's strike force follows the Lucifer into the Delta Serpentis-Sol jump corridor. Terran bombers destroy its reactors in subspace, causing a hull breach. The resulting explosion leads to collapse of the Delta Serpentis-Sol node, isolating Sol from the rest of known space. The Great War ends in Pyrrhic victory — while the Shivans are defeated, the Vasudan homeworld is devastated and the Earth isolated.

FreeSpace 1 campaign is split into 3 acts. Each act contains around 10 missions, as well as cutscenes and high-level command briefings. Outline of the campaign and each mission (with briefings, walkthroughs and debriefings) follows.

This is a very easy mission on medium, but not on the higher difficulties, if you're trying to get the secondary objective of keeping the Galatea above 50 percent hull integrity. Remember that your ML-16s need very little energy. Divert all but one tick power from your primary recharge to your engines. I suggest the Valkyrie interceptor. The faster you are, the more quickly you can get from one side of the Galatea to the other, should the situation require it.

Advice for authoring your own missions/campaigns

Purpose:
This section adds compact implementation advice for mission ideas.

Mission clarity and player agency:
- Give each mission one dominant playable task, then add complications gradually. Exceptional long chapter-ending missions can have multiple main tasks and happen in multiple phases.
- Make objectives measurable: protect a named ship until departure, scan a container, disable engines, disarm turrets, escort at least 3 of 4 transports, keep a cruiser above 50% hull, or reach a Jump Node or a Nav Buoy.
- If a ship or installation is destined to be doomed, make the real objective something the player can accomplish: save escape pods, delay bombers, cover survivors, recover data, or buy time.
- Define partial success for evacuation, convoy, and last-stand missions.

Scale, navigation, and pacing:
- Maximum speed of fighters is about 80 m/s, much lower for larger ships.
- Keep mission points of interest under about 20 km apart whenever possible; 10 km is already a long flight for the player and much longer for larger ships.
- If a slow convoy must cross several kilometers, fill the travel time with meaningful beats: enemy waves, scans, docking, repairs, asteroid hazards, or radio chatter.
- FSO waypoints are hidden to the player and should not be used for in-game player guidance, only for AI paths and logic checks. If you need to guide the player to a specific point in space, it should probably have something interesting visible on radar, such as a ship, a station or a jump node.
- In nebula missions where visibility is limited, you can use a visible navigation buoy (`Terran NavBuoy` or `Vasudan NavBuoy` ship class) for player guidance and to mark important in-game destinations. Keep in mind that the NavBuoys will not be visible on radar beyond the nebula sensor range limit.
- All Great War-era weapon ranges are below 2,000 m. There are no long-range missiles or beams in this era. Keep this in mind when positioning ships that should engage each other.

Simple event logic:
- Prefer triggers based on observable game states: elapsed time, arrivals, departures, destruction, scans, docking or undocking, disabled subsystems, hull thresholds, or a ship reaching a visible marker or finishing a waypoint path.
- In Mission Design Documents describe logic in plain English.
- Avoid abstract conditions the engine cannot see, such as morale, player intent, fear, or complex tactical judgment.
- Every mission should have a clear ending so the player knows when to warp out or dock in a capital ship fighterbay.

Balance reminders:
- Bombers are the main threat to cruisers, destroyers, transports, installations, and escape pods; fighters usually escort, distract, or pressure the player.
- Give the player warning and intercept distance before bomber attacks.
- Stagger enemy waves so several bomber and fighter groups do not overwhelm the player at once.
- Support ships and reinforcements should arrive early enough to matter.
- In more challenging missions allow player to call in 1-2 reinforcement wings with the FSO Reinforcements feature, if the campaign lore allows it.

Useful tips:
- Name exact ship classes, wing names and wing wave counts.
- Describe arrival directions or distances, objectives, message triggers, and debrief outcomes.
- Keep implementation-facing names short and distinct, ideally under 30 characters. FSO does not support very long ship or object names.
- Repeating or inconsistent wing designations (Beta, Aries, Krishna, etc...) in different missions are not an issue; They are just ad-hoc monikers issued by the commander on a per-mission basis, not permanent names.

FSO engine capabilities:
- FSO supported mission environments:
  - normal space
  - dense nebula
    - reduced visibility and sensor range
    - can simulate supernova remnant nebula or gas giant atmosphere
    - nebula colors (can be combined): blue, cyan, green, red, purple, yellow, orange, black
  - subspace tunnel (space inside inter-system connection)
  - asteroid field
    - asteroid fields can be set to actively harass a specific ship, causing damaging impacts unless the incoming rocks are destroyed
  - debris field
    - types: Terran, Vasudan, Shivan
    - debris sizes: small, medium, large
- All ships should be Friendly, Hostile, or Unknown to the player. "Neutral" is not supported in FSO.

Adherence to FreeSpace canon lore:
- If your campaign happens in established setting, you should take care to not violate FreeSpace canon and not include any anachronistic elements:
  - Establish time and place: for each mission, determine where and when: pick a specific system and specific time in the canon timeline.
  - Establish the technological level of GTA forces in your mission:
    - available ships
    - available weapons
    - shields or lack of them
    - do they have sensors that can target the Shivans or not?
  - Keep in mind important canon events and political changes:
    - Terran stance towards Vasudans should be different before the arrival of the Shivans, after their arrival and after the official ceasefire
    - at first, the Shivans are just "Unknowns"; they are named and characterized only after the GTA learns more about them
    - Avenger cannon is only issued fleet-wide some time after the arrival of the Shivans; shields are developed and issued even later, after the technology is captured from the Shivans
    - After the Shivan arrival: initial panic, cautious optimism after getting the Avenger, signing the ceasefire and capturing the shield tech, renewed panic and pessimism after encountering the Lucifer
