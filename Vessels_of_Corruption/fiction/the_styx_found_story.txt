# Fiction Viewer: The Remains of the Styx

The explosion of the GTSC Styx did not look like a ship dying. There were no bright flares of burning oxygen, no clean metal fragments spinning into the void. Instead, it was a rupture. The hull, which had grown thick and black with Shivan matter, tore open like a ripened fruit, spilling a thick, oily cloud of biological detritus that refused to dissipate in the vacuum.

I saw them through my cockpit glass—the things Kael mentioned. Not just faces, but hands. Human hands, fused into the internal bulkheads, reaching for a sky they would never see again. The Styx hadn't just been captured; it had been digested. Every circuit, every crew member, every line of research data had been processed by the Shivan hive. 

As the subspace rift collapsed, the screaming in our comms finally stopped. But the silence that followed was worse. It was the silence of a grave. We are returning to the Erebus, but none of us are talking. We all saw the "Heart"—that pulsating, red core that looked more like an organ than an engine. The Shivans aren't just here to kill us. They're here to use us. And I fear the Erebus might be the next meal.
