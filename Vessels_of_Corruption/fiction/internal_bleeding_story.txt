# Fiction Viewer: The Voices in the Walls

It started on the third day after we entered the Void. A sound like fingernails dragging across the interior of the hull plating. Engineering called it thermal expansion, the stress of the pocket’s "bleed" on the Erebus’s aging spaceframe. We wanted to believe them.

Then the whispers began.

They don't come from the comms, not really. They come from the vibrations of the ship. If you press your ear against a bulkhead in the quiet hours, you can hear them—thousands of voices, speaking a language that sounds like grinding bone. Sarah Vane hasn't slept in forty-eight hours. She says she saw her brother in the mess hall—the one who died during the 14-Year War at Gulnara. He was standing there, his skin the color of a Shivan hull, telling her that the dark is warm.

The medical wing is full. Not with the wounded, but with the screaming. The survivors from the Aethelgard have started to... change. Their eyes are turning black, the pupils dilating until the iris is gone. They keep talking about the "Lucifer" as if it were a god, but they speak of another one, too. The Malphas. The Mother.

I went to my locker today to get my flight suit. There was a thin, black film on the metal. It felt like oil, but it pulsed when I touched it. I didn't tell anyone. We're all pretending not to see it. We're all pretending that we're still human. But as I sit here, writing this, I can hear the walls whispering my name. They want me to open the door. They want the light to come in.
